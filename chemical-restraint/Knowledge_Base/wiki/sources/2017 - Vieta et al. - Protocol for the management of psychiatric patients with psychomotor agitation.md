# Protocol for the management of psychiatric patients with psychomotor agitation

## Summary
This source describes a clinical protocol developed by a multidisciplinary expert panel in Spain for the assessment and management of psychiatric patients experiencing psychomotor agitation (PMA). The protocol integrates international consensus guidelines with local Spanish healthcare standards and hospital-specific Standard Operating Procedures through a formal consensus process using the Delphi method. It provides three clinical algorithms for initial assessment, pharmacological intervention selection, and physical restraint standards, advocating a stepwise approach that prioritizes safety, environmental modifications, verbal de-escalation, and pharmacological treatment before considering seclusion or restraint as last resorts. The protocol aims to standardize care, promote early intervention, reduce reliance on coercive measures, and ensure patient and staff safety through structured, evidence-informed guidance.

## Key Concepts
- **Psychomotor agitation** — A state of motor restlessness and mental tension requiring prompt recognition and management to prevent escalation to aggression
- **Verbal de-escalation** — A therapeutic communication technique using calm interaction and active listening to reduce agitation without confrontation
- **Environmental modifications** — Adjustments to surroundings (light, noise, removal of dangerous objects) to reduce stimuli and promote safety
- **Pharmacological treatment** — Use of medications (inhaled, oral, IM) to rapidly calm patients based on etiology and severity, avoiding over-sedation
- **Physical restraint** — Application of approved mechanical devices to limit patient mobility when behavior poses imminent risk and other strategies fail
- **Delphi method** — Structured communication technique using iterative expert questionnaires to reach consensus on clinical guidelines
- **Jadad scale** — Tool for assessing quality of randomized controlled trials based on randomization, blinding, and dropouts
- **PRISMA statement** — Guidelines for transparent reporting in systematic reviews and meta-analyses
- **Standard Operating Procedures** — Established internal protocols from Hospital Clínic used to adapt international guidelines to local context

## Main Arguments & Findings
- **Core claim**: The protocol provides standardized, locally adapted guidance for identifying and managing psychomotor agitation in psychiatric patients within the Spanish healthcare system, promoting early detection, least invasive interventions, and reduced use of coercive measures.
  - **Evidence**: Developed via systematic review (Jadad, PRISMA), Delphi consensus, and expert panel input; integrated international guidelines with local laws and hospital SOPs; includes three clinical algorithms for assessment, pharmacology, and restraint; recommends sequential approach from least to most restrictive interventions.
  - **Strength**: Moderate to strong — based on formal consensus methods and systematic review, but limited by lack of empirical validation of the protocol itself and reliance on expert opinion without real-world outcome data.

## Contradictions & Tensions
- While the protocol advocates avoiding coercive measures, it provides detailed guidelines for their use when deemed necessary, which may inadvertently normalize such practices despite the stated goal of minimization.
- The requirement to inform authorities about voluntary patients undergoing restraint (even if consented) may conflict with patient autonomy principles in some mental health frameworks, though it aligns with Spanish legal requirements.
