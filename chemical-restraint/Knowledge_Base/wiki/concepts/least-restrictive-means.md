# least restrictive means

## Why It Matters
- Ensures ethical use of restraint by minimizing liberty restrictions while maintaining safety  
- Serves as a legal and clinical safeguard against unnecessary coercion in psychiatric care  
- Aligns with human rights standards requiring proportionality and last-resort application  
- Promotes patient autonomy and recovery by prioritizing least intrusive interventions  

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Ethical standard for restraint | Must minimally restrict liberty, be medically appropriate, and involve consent or emergency exception | [[2022 - Crutchfield, Redinger - The Conditions for Ethical Chemical Restraints]] |
| Critique of current practice | Psychiatric coercion often fails as last resort, lacks therapeutic benefit, and does not restore autonomy | [[Human Rights in Psychiatry - 08]] |
