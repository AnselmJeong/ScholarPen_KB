# Informed Consent
## Why It Matters
- Central to ethical practice in psychiatry and healthcare
- Balances respect for patient autonomy with clinical responsibilities
- Addresses power imbalances in clinical relationships
- Foundational for assessing decision-making capacity and voluntariness
## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|----------|------------|-------------|
| Ethical decision-making requires dialogue and reflection over rigid models | In psychiatry, ethical decision-making should prioritize dialogue, reflection, and attention to relational dynamics rather than strict adherence to fixed ethical models | [[2020 - Adshead, Cave - An introduction to clinical ethics in psychiatry]] |
| Right to refuse medication is a core component of informed consent | Patients possess a fundamental right to refuse psychiatric medication as part of informed consent and personal autonomy | [[1980 - Ford - The Psychiatrist's Double Bind; The Right to Refuse Medication]] |
