# Mental Healthcare Act 2017
## Why It Matters
- Promotes rights-based mental healthcare aligned with UNCRPD standards in India
- Addresses legal capacity and decision-making autonomy for persons with mental illness
- Highlights tensions between safety interventions (e.g., restraints) and patient rights
- Links human rights awareness to treatment adherence and emotional well-being

| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Rights-based approach | The Act aligns with UNCRPD in advancing a rights-based model but faces conceptual tensions regarding legal capacity | [[2019 - Raveesh et al. - How right is right-based mental health law]] |
