# formal coercion

## Why It Matters
- Central to understanding legally sanctioned interventions in mental health care  
- Highlights the tension between patient safety and individual autonomy  
- Informs debates on the ethical and legal justification of compulsory treatment  
- Distinguishes formal mechanisms from informal pressures in clinical practice  

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Formal coercion as legally regulated force | Involves physical force or legally authorized measures under mental health law | [[Coercion and Violence in Mental Health Settings - 08]] |
| Patient experiences vary by context | Safety, improved care quality, and participation reduce perceived coercion despite concerns about forced medication | [[2023 - Brekke et al. - Patients' experiences with coercive mental health treatment in Flexible Assertive Community Treatment a qualitative study]] |
| Formal coercion requires strict ethical conditions | Justifiable only if necessary, proportional, and procedurally just, per harm principle or soft paternalism | [[Coercion and Violence in Mental Health Settings - 08]] |
