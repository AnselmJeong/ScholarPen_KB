# outpatient commitment

## Why It Matters
- Highlights how treatment adherence can be leveraged through non-hospital systems like housing, justice, and welfare  
- Reveals gaps in human rights protections for people with mental illness in Europe despite policy progress  
- Illustrates the shift from institutional to community-based coercive mechanisms  
- Underscores the importance of implementation quality and oversight in determining outcomes  

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Support for mandated community treatment | Uses leverage from housing, justice, and welfare systems to improve treatment adherence, with outcomes dependent on mechanism and implementation quality | [[Coercive Treatment in Psychiatry - 05]] |
| Critique of current protections | Human rights of people with mental illness remain inadequately protected in Europe due to legal inconsistencies, weak enforcement, reinstitutionalization, and lack of monitoring | [[Coercive Treatment in Psychiatry - 11]] |
