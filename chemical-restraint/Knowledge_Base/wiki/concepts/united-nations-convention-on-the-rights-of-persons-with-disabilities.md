# United Nations Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities

## Why It Matters
- Central to understanding human rights frameworks in mental health and disability rights discourse
- Underpins legal capacity and informed consent debates in psychiatric treatment
- Directly referenced in critiques of involuntary treatment and coercive practices
- Provides the normative basis for alternatives like supported decision-making and advance directives

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| CRPD-aligned human rights approach | Mental health law must prioritize autonomy, reject coercion, and ensure access to voluntary, culturally responsive care | [[2020 - Bergan - The Right to Choose and Refuse Mental Health Care: A Human The Right to Choose and Refuse Mental Health Care; A Human Rights Based Approach to Ending Compulsory Psychiatric Intervention]] |
| Human rights-based psychosocial support | Service user preferences must be prioritized over clinical evidence; legal capacity respected; coercion eliminated except for self-binding advance directives | [[Human Rights in Psychiatry - 15]] |
