# five-dimensional dignity

## Why It Matters
- Central to interpreting disability rights under the CRPD, particularly legal capacity  
- Provides a multidimensional framework for balancing autonomy and support  
- Informs ethical and legal approaches to decision-making for people with cognitive disabilities  
- Bridges human rights theory and clinical practice in mental health and disability law  

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Inherent dignity framework | Dignity in the CRPD comprises five dimensions: equal worth, autonomy for the capable, interdependence, embodiment, and indivisibility of rights | [[Mental Capacity, Dignity and the Power of International Human Rights -05]] |
| Interpretation of Article 12 | Article 12 must be read through dignity and indivisibility to permit supported decision-making and restrict substitute decision-making to last resort | [[Mental Capacity, Dignity and the Power of International Human Rights -07]] |
