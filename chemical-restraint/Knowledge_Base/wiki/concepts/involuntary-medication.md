# involuntary medication
## Why It Matters
- Central to ethical and legal debates in psychiatric care due to tensions between patient autonomy and clinical necessity  
- Lacks a universally accepted definition, leading to inconsistent application in clinical and policy contexts  
- Raises concerns about human rights violations, coercion, and potential abuse in psychiatric settings  
- Intersects with broader discussions on consent, capacity, and the use of coercive measures in healthcare
