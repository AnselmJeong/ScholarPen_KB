# advance directives
## Why It Matters
- Reduces coercion by enabling prior consent and aligning care with patient preferences
- Honors enduring will over transient preferences in mental health contexts
- Supports compliance with the UN CRPD's respect for rights, will, and preferences
- Addresses systemic dilemmas in psychiatric care requiring legal and social reforms
## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|---|---|---|
| Advance directives reduce coercion | Enable prior consent to align crisis interventions with patient preferences | [[2012 - Bonnie - Advance Directives A Tool for Reducing Coercion]] |
| Respect for enduring will | CRPD requires distinguishing enduring will from transient preferences; honoring prior will may override current preferences | [[2017 - Szmukler - The UN Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities 'Rights, will and preferences' in relation to mental health disabilities]] |
| Alternatives to coercion | Trauma-informed care, peer support, and shared decision-making reduce coercion across settings | [[Coercion and Violence in Mental Health Settings - 18]] |
| Systemic dilemmas from abolition | Abolishing psychiatric coercion creates challenges in forensic, suicide, and dementia care requiring broader reforms | [[Human Rights in Psychiatry - 17]] |
