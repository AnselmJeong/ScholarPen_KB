# justice

## Why It Matters
- Justice is central to equitable healthcare distribution, especially under conditions of scarcity
- It informs ethical decision-making in clinical and research contexts by addressing fairness in access, burden, and benefit
- Theoretical approaches to justice are debated due to their limitations in resolving complex biomedical dilemmas
- It intersects with principles like beneficence, autonomy, and non-maleficence in shaping professional obligations

## Key Positions
