# substantive equality

## Why It Matters
- Central to interpreting CRPD Article 12 on legal capacity as requiring equality in practice, not just formal rights  
- Bridges civil-political and socio-economic rights through the indivisibility principle  
- Supports dignity by recognizing that autonomy requires both internal capacity and external support  
- Positions supported decision-making as normative and substitute decision-making as a last resort  

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Supported decision-making as norm | Legal capacity "on an equal basis with others" requires inclusive equality, making supported decision-making the standard approach to uphold dignity and substantive equality | [[Mental Capacity, Dignity and the Power of International Human Rights -06]] |
| Substitute decision-making as last resort | Substitute decision-making should only be used for those with severe cognitive impairment when supported decision-making is insufficient, guided by dignity and indivisibility | [[Mental Capacity, Dignity and the Power of International Human Rights -07]] |
| Autonomy as achievement with support | Autonomy under CRPD Article 12 is not innate but achieved through a combination of internal competencies and external support, integrating rights via indivisibility | [[Mental Capacity, Dignity and the Power of International Human Rights -04]] |
| Indivisibility of rights | Civil-political rights (like legal capacity) and socio-economic rights must be treated as interconnected to realize substantive equality under the CRPD | [[Mental Capacity, Dignity and the Power of International Human Rights -04]] |

## Open questions
- How can systems effectively determine when supported decision-making is insufficient and substitute decision-making becomes necessary without undermining the individual’s dignity?  
- What practical mechanisms ensure that support for decision-making is truly accessible and not merely procedural, especially in resource-limited settings?  
- To what extent does the interpretation of "equal basis with others" in CRPD Article 12 require addressing structural socio-economic inequalities beyond individual support measures?  
- How should legal systems balance the indivisibility of rights when socio-economic supports needed for substantive equality are not adequately provided by the state?
