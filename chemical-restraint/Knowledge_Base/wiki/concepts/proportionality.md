# proportionality

## Why It Matters
- Guides ethical decisions on coercion by requiring interventions to be proportionate to risks  
- Balances patient autonomy with safety in psychiatric care  
- Ensures least restrictive alternatives are considered before restrictive measures  
- Grounds clinical ethics in structured, transparent decision-making  

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|----------|------------|-------------|
| Proportionality as structured decision-making | Coercion should follow a structured algorithm based on capacity, benefit-harm analysis, and biopsychosocial context | [[2023 - Steinert - Using coercion in mental disorders or risking the patients death An analysis of the protocols of a clinical ethics committee]] |
| Proportionality through individual preference | Least restrictive restraint must prioritize individual service users' preferences over rigid hierarchies | [[2024 - Hempeler et al. - Preferences of Individual Mental Health Service Users Are Essential in Determining the Least Restrictive Type of Restraint]] |
| Proportionality as last resort | Mechanical restraint is justified only as a last resort for life-threatening self-injury or extreme violence within legal frameworks | [[The Use of Coercive Measures in Forensic Psychiatric Care - 22]] |
