# Mental Health Act
## Why It Matters
- Addresses legal frameworks governing involuntary treatment and detention
- Balances public safety with individual rights in mental healthcare
- Highlights ethical tensions in forensic psychiatric settings
- Informs policy on coercive practices across care pathways

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|----------|------------|-------------|
| Coercion extends beyond formal detention | Informal admissions and community treatments involve subtle pressures increasing across care pathways | [[The Use of Coercive Measures in Forensic Psychiatric Care - 08]] |
| Mechanical restraint as last resort | Ethically justified in high-secure settings for life-threatening self-injury or extreme violence under strict governance | [[The Use of Coercive Measures in Forensic Psychiatric Care - 22]] |
