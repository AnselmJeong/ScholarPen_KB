# meta-regression
## Why It Matters
- Explains heterogeneity in pooled estimates across studies
- Identifies sources of variability in prevalence rates of restrictive practices
- Supports evidence-based policy by linking study characteristics to outcomes
- Enhances generalizability of meta-analyses in mental health and aged care research

## Open questions
- What standardized measurement tools could reduce heterogeneity in future meta-analyses?
- How do cultural and legal contexts interact to influence restrictive practice use beyond what is captured by region alone?
- Can meta-regression identify threshold effects where certain study characteristics dramatically alter outcomes?
- Are there moderating variables (e.g., staff training, unit size) not yet explored in current meta-regression models?
