# evidence-based medicine

## Why It Matters
- Central to evaluating clinical practices in psychiatry, including coercive interventions  
- Highlighted as insufficient alone to establish universal standards for coercive treatment  
- Intersects with ethical frameworks like virtue ethics and consensus in clinical decision-making  
- Frequently cited in debates about justification and limits of psychiatric interventions  

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Evidence-based medicine has limits in psychiatry | Cannot define objective, universally applicable best practice standards for coercive treatment when used in isolation | [[Coercive Treatment in Psychiatry - 06]] |
| Appeals to autonomy and dignity insufficient alone | These principles can support or oppose seclusion depending on interpretation and context | [[2008 - Prinsen et al. - Can we justify eliminating coercive measures in psychiatry]] |
