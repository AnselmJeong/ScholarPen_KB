# ethical decision-making

## Why It Matters
- Central to clinical ethics in psychiatry when balancing patient autonomy and safety  
- Highlights limitations of principle-based frameworks in complex clinical scenarios  
- Informs structured approaches to coercion and involuntary treatment decisions  
- Addresses systemic issues like power imbalances and jurisdictional inconsistencies  

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Structured algorithm approach | Ethical decisions about coercion should follow a stepwise process using capacity, benefit-harm analysis, and biopsychosocial context | [[2023 - Steinert - Using coercion in mental disorders or risking the patients death An analysis of the protocols of a clinical ethics committee]] |
| Principlism limitations | Principlism fails in chemical restraint decisions due to overemphasis on autonomy, lack of conflict resolution theory, and neglect of moral community impact | [[2024 - Mangal, Scheiner - The Limitations of Principlism]] |
| Contextual barriers in involuntary treatment | Ethical decision-making is undermined by power imbalances, poor communication, and inconsistent criteria across regions | [[2024 - Laureano et al. - Ethical Issues in Clinical Decision-Making about Involuntary Psychiatric Treatment A Scoping Review]] |
