# clinical ethics

## Why It Matters
- Central to ethical decision-making in psychiatric clinical practice  
- Guides the use and limitation of coercive interventions in mental health care  
- Addresses tensions between patient autonomy and clinician duty of care  
- Informs policy and guidelines for ethical standards in psychiatry globally  

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Ethical use of coercion | Coercion in psychiatry should follow structured decision algorithms based on capacity, benefit-harm analysis, and biopsychosocial context | [[2023 - Steinert - Using coercion in mental disorders or risking the patients death An analysis of the protocols of a clinical ethics committee]] |
| Global psychiatric ethics framework | The WPA Code of Ethics for Psychiatry provides a living document grounded in five adapted medical ethics principles to guide clinical practice, education, research, and advocacy | [[2021 - Appelbaum, Tyano - The WPA Code of Ethics for Psychiatry]] |
