# forensic mental health

## Why It Matters
- Explores coercive practices in mental health care  
- Examines human rights implications in forensic settings  
- Investigates technology use for safety and risk assessment  
- Addresses legal and ethical frameworks for involuntary treatment  

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Human rights framework for reducing coercion | Integrates human rights principles with family involvement and legal reform to minimize coercive measures in mental health care | [[2013 - Lepping, Raveesh - The Mysore Declaration]] |
| Staff perspectives on remote monitoring for aggression | Passive remote monitoring seen as useful for early aggression detection but hindered by safety, privacy, workload, and engagement barriers | [[2019 - Greer et al. - Predicting Inpatient Aggression in Forensic Services Using Remote Monitoring Technology Qualitative Study of Staff Perspectives]] |
