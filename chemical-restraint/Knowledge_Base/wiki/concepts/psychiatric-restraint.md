# psychiatric restraint

## Why It Matters
- A globally prevalent coercive practice in mental healthcare with significant ethical and clinical implications  
- Associated with measurable worsening of mental health outcomes, even in controlled settings  
- Varies widely across economic contexts, from regulated forms in high-income countries to extreme unregulated practices like pasung  
- Central to debates on human rights, autonomy, and the ethics of coercion in psychiatry  

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Clinical/economic variation | Coercion is globally pervasive but takes legalized forms in high-income countries and extreme, unregulated forms like pasung in low/middle-income countries | [[2017 - Molodynski et al. - Key international themes in coercion]] |
| Harm of coercive measures | Exposure to any coercive measure during psychiatric hospitalization is associated with a small but statistically significant worsening of mental health at discharge | [[2024 - Baggio et al. - Effect of coercive measures on mental health status in adult psychiatric populations a nationwide trial emulation]] |
