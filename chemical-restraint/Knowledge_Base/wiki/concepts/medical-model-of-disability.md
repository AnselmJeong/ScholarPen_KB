# medical model of disability
## Why It Matters
- Central to understanding critiques of psychiatric practices in the archive
- Underpins arguments about involuntary treatment and legal capacity
- Contrasted with rights-based approaches like those in the CRPD
- Key to analyzing ethical and human rights implications in mental health care
## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|----------|------------|-------------|
| Medical model of disability | Views disability as an individual pathology requiring medical intervention | [[2019 - Puras et al. - Mental health and human rights in the 21st century]], [[Human Rights in Psychiatry - 05]] |
| Critique of medical model | Argues it justifies coercion by locating disability solely in the individual | [[2019 - Puras et al. - Mental health and human rights in the 21st century]], [[Human Rights in Psychiatry - 05]] |
| Human rights alternative | Advocates for rights-based models centered on autonomy and legal capacity | [[2019 - Puras et al. - Mental health and human rights in the 21st century]], [[Human Rights in Psychiatry - 05]] |
