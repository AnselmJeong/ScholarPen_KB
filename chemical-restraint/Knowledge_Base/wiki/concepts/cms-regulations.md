# CMS regulations

## Why It Matters
- Central to legal and ethical boundaries in psychiatric care
- Governs permissible use of restraint and seclusion
- Requires compliance with federal oversight and due process
- Shapes clinical decision-making during behavioral emergencies

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Legal compliance | Seclusion and restraint are legally permissible only as a last resort, subject to federal, state, and accreditation regulations | [[Clinical Manual of Psychiatry and Law - 06]] |
| Clinical practice shift | Physical restraint has become a highly regulated last-resort intervention due to patient rights advocacy and documented harms | [[2017 - Masters - Physical Restraint A Historical Review and Current Practice]] |
| Preventive leadership | Psychiatrists should lead reduction efforts through the Six Core Strategies, moving beyond reactive orders to team-based prevention | [[2020 - Masters et al. - The Role of the Psychiatrist in Seclusion and Restraint]] |
