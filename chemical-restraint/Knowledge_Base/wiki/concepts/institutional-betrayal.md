# institutional betrayal

## Why It Matters
- Highlights systemic failures in institutional accountability within mental health settings  
- Explains how harm is enabled through underreporting and inadequate responses despite known risks  
- Connects institutional power dynamics to patient experiences of betrayal and mistrust  
- Underscores ethical and human rights implications of coercive practices in care environments  

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Systemic harm enabled by institutional failure | Abuse occurs due to underreporting, power imbalances, and stigma, not individual malice alone | [[Coercion and Violence in Mental Health Settings - 02]] |
| Coercion as structurally driven, not clinically necessary | Harmful practices stem from understaffing and cost-cutting, not patient needs | [[The Use of Coercive Measures in Forensic Psychiatric Care - 20]] |
