# recovery model
## Why It Matters
- The recovery model is a foundational concept in mental health advocacy and policy reform.
- It challenges coercive frameworks by emphasizing person-centered, rights-based approaches.
- It aligns with human rights instruments like the CRPD and ethical principles of autonomy and dignity.
- It informs alternatives to coercive practices in psychiatric care.

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Recovery-oriented care | Recovery is a personal journey of healing and self-determination, not merely clinical symptom reduction | [[Coercive Treatment in Psychiatry - 09]] |
| Recovery-oriented care | Recovery requires addressing systemic injustices and power imbalances in psychiatric systems | [[The Use of Coercive Measures in Forensic Psychiatric Care - 20]] |
| Recovery-oriented care | True recovery is incompatible with coercive interventions that undermine autonomy and dignity | [[Coercive Treatment in Psychiatry - 09]], [[The Use of Coercive Measures in Forensic Psychiatric Care - 20]] |
