# principle of indivisibility

## Why It Matters
- Shapes interpretation of CRPD Article 12 regarding legal capacity
- Balances supported and substitute decision-making for persons with cognitive disabilities
- Links dignity with equality and social inclusion in disability rights
- Informs human rights-based approaches to mental health law

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|----------|------------|-------------|
| Supported by interpretation of CRPD | Article 12 requires interpretation through indivisibility and dignity to allow substitute decision-making as last resort | [[Mental Capacity, Dignity and the Power of International Human Rights -03]] |
| Supported by interpretation of CRPD | Article 12 should allow both supported and substitute decision-making as last resort for severe cognitive impairment | [[Mental Capacity, Dignity and the Power of International Human Rights -07]] |
