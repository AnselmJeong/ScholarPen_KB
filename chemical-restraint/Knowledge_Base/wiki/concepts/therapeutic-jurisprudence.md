# therapeutic jurisprudence

## Why It Matters
- Explores how legal processes and courtroom practices influence psychological well-being and therapeutic outcomes
- Addresses ethical tensions between legal authority and mental health care delivery
- Highlights the role of law in either alleviating or exacerbating psychological distress
- Informs reforms aimed at aligning legal systems with mental health recovery and human rights

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Therapeutic jurisprudence as evaluative framework | Laws and legal procedures should be assessed for their therapeutic or anti-therapeutic impact on individuals | [[Clinical Handbook of Psychiatry and Law - 03]] |
| Law as potential source of harm | Involuntary commitment and treatment often fail to provide adequate care and may violate rights, especially when driven by systemic underfunding | [[Clinical Handbook of Psychiatry and Law - 03]] |
| Human rights imperative | Denial of treatment refusal violates constitutional and international rights; advocates for supported decision-making over coercion | [[2024 - Bergan - The Right to Choose and Refuse Mental Health Care; A Human Rights Based Approach to Ending Compulsory Psychiatric Intervention]] |
| Ethical decision-making in teams | Resolving clinical ethics disagreements requires attention to emotions, social dynamics, and reflective practice—not just logic | [[2017 - Silva et al. - Ethical dilemmas in psychiatry; When teams disagree]] |

## Open questions
- How can legal systems better distinguish between therapeutic and anti-therapeutic applications of coercion in mental health care?
- What mechanisms ensure that the right to refuse treatment is respected without compromising safety or public health obligations?
- In what ways do implicit biases and institutional cultures undermine the therapeutic intent of mental health laws?
- How might supported decision-making models be scaled to replace substitute decision-making in civil commitment proceedings?
