# Community Treatment Orders
## Why It Matters
- Central to debates on balancing patient autonomy with public safety in mental health law  
- Represents a coercive intervention that extends beyond hospitalization into community settings  
- Highlights tensions between legal frameworks and therapeutic goals in psychiatric care  
- Serves as a focal point for human rights critiques of psychiatric practices  

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|----------|------------|-------------|
| Critique of coercion efficacy | Psychiatric coercion, including community treatment orders, fails to benefit most patients and lacks evidence for restoring autonomy | [[Human Rights in Psychiatry - 08]] |
| Law-psychiatry interdependence | The relationship between law and psychiatry is bidirectional, shaped by evolving societal values like patient autonomy, not legal dominance | [[2022 - Steinert et al. - Law and psychiatry--current and future perspectives]] |
| Measurement and definition challenges | International efforts to reduce coercion are hindered by inconsistent definitions and measurement, despite consensus on core concepts | [[Coercion and Violence in Mental Health Settings - 05]] |
