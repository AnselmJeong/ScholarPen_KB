# Involuntary hospitalization

## Why It Matters
- Central to understanding coercive practices in mental health care
- Highlights tensions between clinical necessity and patient autonomy
- Reflects broader systemic issues in mental health service delivery
- Intersects with human rights frameworks and legal standards

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Ethical concern | Involuntary hospitalization raises significant ethical challenges regarding autonomy and consent | [[Coercion and Violence in Mental Health Settings - 05]] |
| Clinical rationale | Legally mandated treatment may be necessary for safety and treatment adherence in severe mental illness | [[2020 - Plahouras et al. - Experiences with legally mandated treatment in patients with schizophrenia A systematic review of qualitative studies]] |
| Human rights perspective | Involuntary measures must comply with international human rights standards, particularly CRPD | [[Coercion and Violence in Mental Health Settings - 05]] |
| Measurement challenge | Inconsistent definitions hinder cross-study comparison and policy evaluation | [[Coercion and Violence in Mental Health Settings - 05]] |
