# tardive dyskinesia

## Why It Matters
- Linked to long-term antipsychotic use and patient autonomy concerns
- Highlights ethical dilemmas in involuntary medication and treatment consent
- Represents a significant adverse effect impacting quality of life and dignity
- Raises legal and clinical questions about risk-benefit assessment in psychiatric care

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Constitutional rights perspective | Involuntarily hospitalized patients have a qualified right to refuse antipsychotics, overridable only if incompetent or dangerous | [[1980 - Brooks - The constitutional right to refuse antipsychotic medications.]] |
| Clinical liability perspective | Psychiatric malpractice arises from deviation from standard care; adherence to sound practice supports risk management | [[Clinical Manual of Psychiatry and Law - 05]] |
