# PROSPERO

## Why It Matters
- Represents a systematic review framework used in evidence synthesis for mental health interventions  
- Highlights methodological rigor in evaluating restrictive practices and acute disturbance management  
- Connects to ethical debates on coercion, safety, and patient experience in psychiatric care  
- Serves as a reference point for comparative effectiveness research in emergency psychiatry  

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| On restrictive practices | Experienced as psychologically harmful by patients and staff, despite staff viewing them as sometimes unavoidable | [[2022 - Butterworth et al. - Patients’ and staff members’ experiences of restrictive practices in acute mental health in-patient settings; systematic review and thematic synthesis]] |
| On IV medications for acute disturbance | Show mixed but positive effectiveness with generally favorable safety profiles, though evidence is limited by heterogeneity and small samples | [[2024 - Castro et al. - Effectiveness and Safety of Intravenous Medications for the Management of Acute Disturbance (Agitation and Other Escalating Behaviors); A Systematic Review of Prospective Interventional Studies]] |

## Open questions
- How can the evidence base for IV medications in acute disturbance be improved given current limitations in study size and heterogeneity?  
- What alternatives to restrictive practices can reduce psychological harm while maintaining safety in psychiatric inpatient settings?  
- How do staff perceptions of necessity around restrictive practices influence their use despite known harms?  
- What role do patient-centered approaches play in mitigating negative experiences of coercive interventions?
