# mental illness stigma

## Why It Matters
- Central to understanding public attitudes that drive approval of coercive psychiatric measures  
- Interacts with ethical tensions in mental health law and practice  
- Influences policy debates around involuntary treatment and human rights protections  
- Shapes patient experiences of care, autonomy, and dignity in psychiatric settings  

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| Stigma drives coercion approval | Public support for psychiatric coercion increases with perceived dangerousness and is amplified by stigmatizing attitudes, especially under severe measures | [[2022 - Steiger et al. - Approval of Coercion in Psychiatry in Public Perception and the Role of Stigmatization]] |
| Ethical split in psychiatry | Psychiatry faces unique ethical conflict due to utilitarian mental health legislation coexisting with rights-based laws, unlike other medical specialties | [[2008 - Lepping - Is psychiatry torn in different ethical directions]] |
