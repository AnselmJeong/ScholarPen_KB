# forced medication

## Why It Matters
- Central to ethical debates on autonomy and consent in psychiatric care  
- Reflects legal and clinical tensions between treatment efficacy and patient rights  
- Predictive factors reveal systemic biases in application (e.g., gender, diagnosis)  
- Associated with measurable negative outcomes post-discharge despite clinical intent  

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Predictive factors in non-emergency use | Female gender, psychotic disorder, and involuntary admission predict forced medication; symptom burden and aggression do not | [[2023 - Meroni et al. - Treatment without consent in adult psychiatry inpatient units a retrospective study on predictive factors]] |
