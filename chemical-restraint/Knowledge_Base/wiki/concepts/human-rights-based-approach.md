# human rights-based approach

## Why It Matters
- Addresses systemic issues like coercion and stigma in psychiatric care through rights-based frameworks  
- Demonstrates practical impact in reducing coercive measures while promoting patient and staff empowerment  
- Highlights gaps in legal protection for mental health rights in low-resource and conflict-affected settings  
- Connects ethical practice with policy change, supporting regional and international reform efforts

| Position | Core claim | Key sources |

- How do intersecting forms of stigma (e.g., based on race, gender, or disability) affect the realization of mental health rights in practice?  
- What balance between autonomy and protection is ethically and legally justifiable in involuntary care under a human rights framework?
