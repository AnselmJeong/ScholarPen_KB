# proportionality principle

## Why It Matters
- Determines ethical limits of coercive psychiatric interventions  
- Balances individual rights against therapeutic and safety imperatives  
- Serves as a legal and human rights safeguard in psychiatric practice  
- Ensures interventions are no more restrictive than necessary  

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Ethical permissibility | Coercion is ethically acceptable only in exceptional cases when it serves higher values like long-term autonomy or safety, after a transparent risk-benefit analysis | [[2021 - Chieze et al. - Coercive Measures in Psychiatry A Review of Ethical Arguments]] |
| Legal and human rights compliance | Involuntary treatment and commitment must adhere to proportionality, necessity, and procedural safeguards under international human rights law to prevent violations of liberty and privacy | [[The Use of Coercive Measures in Forensic Psychiatric Care - 03]] |
