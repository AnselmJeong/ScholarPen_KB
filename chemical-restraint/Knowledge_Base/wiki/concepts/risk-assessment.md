# risk assessment

## Why It Matters
- Enables prediction and prevention of imminent aggression in clinical settings  
- Balances safety imperatives with ethical and legal obligations in patient care  
- Informs decisions about coercive measures through structured, evidence-based tools  
- Supports minimization of restraint by identifying alternatives like de-escalation  

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|----------|------------|-------------|
| Structured tools predict imminent aggression | Brøset Violence Checklist and DASA show large effect sizes for 24-hour aggression prediction | [[2020 - Dickens et al. - Structured assessments for imminent aggression in mental health and correctional settings Systematic review and meta-analysis]] |
| Machine learning enhances prediction | Gradient boosting models show moderate to strong performance for violent behavior in schizophrenia, but lack external validation | [[2024 - Parsaei et al. - Machine Learning for prediction of violent behaviors in schizophrenia spectrum disorders a systematic review]] |
| Remote monitoring has potential but barriers | Staff see passive remote monitoring useful for early detection but cite safety, privacy, workload, and engagement challenges | [[2019 - Greer et al. - Predicting Inpatient Aggression in Forensic Services Using Remote Monitoring Technology Qualitative Study of Staff Perspectives]] |
| Staff knowledge gaps persist | Nurses have moderate overall knowledge of restraint but significant deficits in legal/ethical understanding despite safety beliefs | [[2016 - Cunha et al. - Chemical and Physical Restraint of Patients]] |
| Restraint poses significant risks | Physical and psychological harm, autonomy violations necessitate minimization via safeguards and de-escalation | [[2024 - Paul et al. - Exploring psychiatric patient restraints Balancing safety, ethics, and patient rights in mental healthcare]] |
| Coercion should be minimized | De-escalation techniques prevent aggression by addressing underlying needs before violence escalates | [[Coercive Treatment in Psychiatry - 07]] |
| Coercion as last resort | In forensic psychiatry, coercive measures remain necessary in extreme situations despite preference for prevention and training | [[The Use of Coercive Measures in Forensic Psychiatric Care - 21]] |
