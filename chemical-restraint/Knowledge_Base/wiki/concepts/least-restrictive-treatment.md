# least restrictive treatment

## Why It Matters
- Ensures patient liberty is prioritized in psychiatric treatment settings  
- Provides a legal and ethical framework for minimizing coercion in mental healthcare  
- Supports the development of alternatives to involuntary interventions  
- Balances treatment efficacy with human rights protections in clinical practice  

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Legal standard for civil commitment | Requires "clear and convincing" evidence of mental illness and dangerousness or grave disability | [[Psychiatry and the Law - 06]] |
| Framework for reducing coercion | Integrates human rights principles with contextual factors like family involvement and legal reform to define and reduce coercive measures | [[2013 - Lepping, Raveesh - The Mysore Declaration]] |
