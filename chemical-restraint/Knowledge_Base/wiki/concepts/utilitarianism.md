# utilitarianism
## Why It Matters
- Utilitarianism shapes mental health legislation like the Mental Health Act 2007, creating ethical tensions with rights-based frameworks  
- It influences resource allocation in healthcare under scarcity, where maximizing aggregate welfare competes with individual rights  
- Highlights a unique ethical split in psychiatry between consequence-driven laws and principle-based human rights instruments  
- Central to debates on coercion, compulsory treatment, and balancing public safety with individual liberty in mental health care

## Open questions
- How can utilitarian principles in mental health law be reconciled with rights-based protections under the Human Rights Act 1998 and CRPD?  
- What safeguards prevent utilitarian justifications from overriding individual autonomy in coercive psychiatric practices?  
- In resource-limited settings, how should utilitarian triage be balanced against principles of equity and non-discrimination?  
- Is a purely consequentialist approach sufficient for assessing the long-term ethical impact of psychiatric interventions?
