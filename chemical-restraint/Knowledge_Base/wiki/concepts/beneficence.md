# Beneficence
## Why It Matters
- Central to biomedical ethics as a guiding principle for promoting patient well-being
- Requires dynamic balancing with autonomy, not merely overriding it in paternalistic interventions
- Highlights limitations of legal approaches to enforcing autonomy in clinical contexts
- Emphasizes relational and contextual dimensions of ethical decision-making in healthcare
