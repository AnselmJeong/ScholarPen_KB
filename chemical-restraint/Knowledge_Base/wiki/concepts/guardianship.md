# Guardianship

## Why It Matters
- Central to legal frameworks governing medical decision-making for individuals lacking capacity  
- Directly intersects with ethical principles of autonomy, beneficence, and paternalism in psychiatric care  
- Key mechanism for implementing legally mandated treatment in severe mental illness  
- Raises critical questions about substituted judgment vs. best interests standards

## Open questions
- How can guardianship systems balance protection of vulnerable individuals with respect for evolving expressions of autonomy?  
- What evidence supports the effectiveness of guardianship in improving long-term outcomes for individuals with schizophrenia compared to less restrictive alternatives?  
- To what extent do cultural and systemic biases influence guardianship appointments and decision-making in psychiatric contexts?  
- How should advance directives interact with guardianship authority when a person's prior wishes conflict with current clinical assessments?
