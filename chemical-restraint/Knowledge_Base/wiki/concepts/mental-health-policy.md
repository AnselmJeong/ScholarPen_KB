# mental health policy
## Why It Matters
- Addresses systemic gaps in mental health care while balancing evidence-based treatment and human rights protections  
- Examines how policy frameworks influence coercive practices such as restraint and involuntary treatment  
- Informs ethical and legal standards for patient autonomy and safety in psychiatric settings  
- Highlights tensions between biomedical approaches and critiques of psychiatric power structures

## Open questions
- How can mental health policies effectively reduce coercive practices without compromising patient or staff safety?  
- What balance should be struck between respecting international human rights critiques and defending the legitimacy of biomedical psychiatry in treatment-resistant cases?  
- To what extent should staff training be mandated and funded as a core component of mental health policy reform?  
- How do cultural and systemic factors influence the implementation of evidence-based policies across different national contexts?
