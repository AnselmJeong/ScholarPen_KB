# psychiatric diagnosis

## Why It Matters
- Central to debates on legitimacy of psychiatric coercion and involuntary treatment  
- Influences public perception of dangerousness and treatment efficacy in coercion debates  
- Linked to diagnostic uncertainty affecting human rights protections in psychiatry  
- Shapes ethical and legal frameworks around capacity assessment and informed consent  

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Diagnostic uncertainty undermines coercion justification | Psychiatric diagnoses lack sufficient reliability to justify coercive measures, violating human rights | [[Human Rights in Psychiatry - 03]] |
| Public acceptance of coercion depends on diagnosis-linked perceptions | Approval of psychiatric coercion driven by perceived dangerousness and treatment usefulness, which are shaped by diagnostic labels | [[2022 - Steiger et al. - Approval of Coercion in Psychiatry in Public Perception and the Role of Stigmatization]] |
