# person-centered care

## Why It Matters
- Aligns with ethical priorities in psychiatric care by centering autonomy and dignity  
- Counters systemic issues like power imbalances and inconsistent application of criteria  
- Supports efforts to minimize coercion through respect for individual preferences  
- Provides framework for balancing beneficence and non-maleficence in involuntary treatment  

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| Ethical decision-making requires balancing autonomy with beneficence, non-maleficence, and justice | Coercion in psychiatry is ethically justified only when these principles are balanced, but power imbalances and poor communication undermine this balance | [[2024 - Laureano et al. - Ethical Issues in Clinical Decision-Making about Involuntary Psychiatric Treatment A Scoping Review]] |

| Coercive measures should be minimized as a last resort | In forensic psychiatric care, coercive measures are sometimes necessary in extreme situations but should be reduced through primary prevention and staff training | [[The Use of Coercive Measures in Forensic Psychiatric Care - 21]] |
