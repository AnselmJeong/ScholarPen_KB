# Coercive measures in psychiatry

## Why It Matters
- Central to ethical and legal debates about patient autonomy in psychiatric care  
- Directly impacts human rights compliance and clinical practice guidelines  
- Reflects tension between therapeutic intent and coercive intervention  
- Influences policy development around involuntary treatment and restraint  

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|---------|-----------|------------|
| Critical abolitionist | Coercive measures cannot be justified and should be eliminated | [[2008 - Prinsen, Delden - Can we justify eliminating coercive measures in psychiatry]] |
| Pragmatic acceptance in forensic settings | Coercive measures are sometimes necessary in forensic psychiatric care for safety and treatment | [[The Use of Coercive Measures in Forensic Psychiatric Care - 03]] |

- How can alternatives to coercion be effectively implemented and scaled across mental health systems?  
- What are the long-term psychological and relational impacts of coercive interventions on patients?  
- How do cultural, legal, and institutional contexts shape the acceptability and use of coercive measures?
