# disability rights

## Why It Matters
- Central to understanding autonomy and legal capacity for persons with disabilities under international human rights law
- Explores the interpretation of CRPD Article 12 regarding decision-making capacity and support systems
- Connects civil-political rights with socio-economic rights through the principle of indivisibility
- Informs ethical and legal frameworks around supported vs. substitute decision-making

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Supported decision-making as primary | Autonomy under CRPD Article 12 is achieved through internal competencies and external support, not replaced by substitute decision-making | [[Mental Capacity, Dignity and the Power of International Human Rights -04]], [[Mental Capacity, Dignity and the Power of International Human Rights -07]] |
| Substitute decision-making as last resort | Should only be used for those with severe cognitive impairment when supported decision-making is not feasible | [[Mental Capacity, Dignity and the Power of International Human Rights -07]] |
| Indivisibility of rights | Civil-political and socio-economic rights must be interpreted together to uphold dignity and autonomy | [[Mental Capacity, Dignity and the Power of International Human Rights -04]], [[Mental Capacity, Dignity and the Power of International Human Rights -07]] |
