# antipsychotics

## Why It Matters
- Central to acute agitation management protocols
- Ethical concerns arise with covert administration in noncompliant patients
- Risk of extrapyramidal symptoms limits long-term use
- Gaps in guidance for sequential treatment after first-line failure

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| First-line for acute agitation | Haloperidol ± anticholinergics preferred due to evidence; benzodiazepines less effective and higher risk | [[2019 - Zareifopoulos et al. - Treatment Options for Acute Agitation in Psychiatric Patients Theoretical and Empirical Evidence]] |
| Sequential treatment gap | Lack of guidelines for persistent agitation after first-line failure; need for risk-informed, stepwise algorithms | [[2021 - Searles Quick et al. - Which Emergent Medication Should I Give Next Repeated Use of Emergent Medications to Treat Acute Agitation]] |
| Covert use ethical tension | Covert antipsychotics may reduce harmful behaviors but fail psychosis control and cause EPS, raising autonomy vs. beneficence conflict | [[2024 - Razzak et al. - Ethical Dilemmas in Covert Prescribing A Case Report on Medication Administration for Noncompliant Psychiatric Patients]] |
