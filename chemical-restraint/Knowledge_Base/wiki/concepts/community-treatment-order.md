# Community Treatment Order

## Why It Matters
- Evaluates effectiveness of compulsory community-based treatment across diagnostic groups
- Reveals differential outcomes: reduced admissions for psychotic disorders but increased use in other conditions
- Highlights ethical tensions between coercion and recovery-oriented care
- Demonstrates need for diagnosis-specific policy in mental health legislation

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Diagnostic-specific efficacy | Community Treatment Orders reduce psychiatric admissions only for psychotic disorders | [[2022 - Beaglehole et al. - Impact of diagnosis on outcomes for compulsory treatment orders in New Zealand]] |
| Broader coercive trend | Use of coercive measures, including community treatment, is rising across the care pathway in England/Wales | [[The Use of Coercive Measures in Forensic Psychiatric Care - 08]] |
| Limited overall benefit | Reductions in admissions are offset by increased antipsychotic use in non-psychotic groups | [[2022 - Beaglehole et al. - Impact of diagnosis on outcomes for compulsory treatment orders in New Zealand]] |
| Systemic persistence | Despite policy goals to reduce coercion, informal and formal coercive practices are increasing | [[The Use of Coercive Measures in Forensic Psychiatric Care - 08]] |
