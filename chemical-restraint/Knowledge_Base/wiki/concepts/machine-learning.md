# machine learning

## Why It Matters
- Enables early prediction of behavioral events in clinical populations
- Supports proactive interventions before crises escalate
- Enhances risk assessment tools for psychiatric conditions
- Requires careful validation before clinical deployment

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Predictive utility | ML models predict challenging or violent behaviors with moderate to strong accuracy | [[2023 - Gifford et al. - Wearable Technology--Another Tool in the Assessment and Treatment of Challenging Behavior Toolbox]], [[2024 - Parsaei et al. - Machine Learning for prediction of violent behaviors in schizophrenia spectrum disorders a systematic review]] |
| Complementary role | ML should support, not replace, established clinical assessment methods | [[2023 - Gifford et al. - Wearable Technology--Another Tool in the Assessment and Treatment of Challenging Behavior Toolbox]] |
| Limitations in generalizability | Heterogeneity in outcomes and lack of external validation restrict real-world use | [[2024 - Parsaei et al. - Machine Learning for prediction of violent behaviors in schizophrenia spectrum disorders a systematic review]] |
