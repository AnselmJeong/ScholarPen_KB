# therapeutic privilege
## Why It Matters
- Addresses ethical tensions in psychiatric decision-making when disclosure may harm the patient  
- Challenges assumptions about universal applicability of informed consent standards  
- Highlights cultural and contextual variability in clinical ethics  
- Intersects with legal standards on capacity and substituted consent  

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Contextual ethics | Ethical principles in psychiatry depend on cultural, religious, economic, and political settings | [[2020 - Atakora, Asampong - Ethical Principles in Psychiatric Practice; A Systematic Review]] |
| Functional competency | Competence for consent is assessed functionally, not by diagnosis, and refusal rights are qualified by law | [[Clinical Manual of Psychiatry and Law - 04]] |
