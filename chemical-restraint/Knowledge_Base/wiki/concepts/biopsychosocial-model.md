# biopsychosocial model

## Why It Matters
- Provides a framework for integrating biological, psychological, and social factors in mental health assessment and intervention
- Challenges purely biomedical approaches by emphasizing context, autonomy, and social determinants of health
- Supports ethical decision-making in coercive practices by requiring consideration of patient context beyond clinical symptoms
- Aligns with human rights frameworks that demand holistic, person-centered care in mental health services
