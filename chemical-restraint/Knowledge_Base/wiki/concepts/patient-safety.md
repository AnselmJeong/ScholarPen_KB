# patient safety

## Why It Matters
- Ensures patient well-being by preventing harm from coercive interventions  
- Addresses systemic failures in reporting and addressing staff violence in mental health settings  
- Highlights gaps in staff knowledge regarding legal and ethical aspects of restraint use  
- Balances safety imperatives with respect for patient rights and autonomy  

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Staff beliefs on safety | Nurses perceive physical and chemical restraint as beneficial for safety despite knowledge gaps | [[2016 - Cunha et al. - Chemical and Physical Restraint of Patients]] |
