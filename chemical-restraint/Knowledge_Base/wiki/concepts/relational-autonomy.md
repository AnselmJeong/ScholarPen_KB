# Relational Autonomy

## Why It Matters
- Central to ethical decision-making in psychiatry, emphasizing relational dynamics over individualistic autonomy models  
- Challenges assumptions of isolated decision-making in clinical ethics frameworks  
- Addresses identity complexities and power imbalances in psychiatric care  
- Supports move away from rigid ethical models toward dialogical and reflective practices  

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Relational autonomy challenges individualistic models | Ethical decision-making must account for social relations, identity, and context, not just autonomous agents | [[2020 - Adshead, Cave - An introduction to clinical ethics in psychiatry]] |
| Critique of coercive measures | Questions justification of coercion in psychiatry by highlighting how autonomy is shaped relationally | [[2008 - Prinsen, Delden - Can we justify eliminating coercive measures in psychiatry]] |
| Emphasis on dialogue and reflection | Ethical reasoning in psychiatry should prioritize process over fixed principles, especially in ambiguous cases | [[2020 - Adshead, Cave - An introduction to clinical ethics in psychiatry]] |
