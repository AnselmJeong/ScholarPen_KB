# prevalence

## Why It Matters
- Quantifies use of restrictive practices across healthcare settings
- Reveals geographic and methodological variability in prevalence estimates
- Highlights ethical concerns regarding patient autonomy and safety
- Informs policy debates on reduction of coercive interventions

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|----------|------------|-------------|
| High prevalence in aged care | Pooled prevalence of physical and chemical restraints is ~33% in residential aged care | [[2020 - Lee et al. - Prevalence and variability in use of physical and chemical restraints in residential aged care facilities; A systematic review and meta-analysis]] |
| Lower but persistent use in mental health | Chemical restraint used in 7.4% of psychiatric/emergency admissions globally; 43.1% of restrained individuals receive it | [[2020 - Muir-Cochrane et al. - Prevalence of the use of chemical restraint in the management of challenging behaviours associated with adult mental health conditions A]] |
| Moderate prevalence in inpatient mental health | Pooled prevalence: physical restraint 14.4%, seclusion 15.8%, chemical restraint 25.7% in adult mental health inpatient settings | [[2024 - Belayneh et al. - Prevalence and variability of restrictive care practice use (physical restraint, seclusion and chemical restraint) in adult mental health inpatient settings]] |
| Extreme heterogeneity across studies | I² >99% in mental health inpatient studies, driven by geography and data collection methods | [[2024 - Belayneh et al. - Prevalence and variability of restrictive care practice use (physical restraint, seclusion and chemical restraint) in adult mental health inpatient settings]] |
| Variability explained by measurement and region | Differences in prevalence linked to assessment tools and regional practices | [[2020 - Lee et al. - Prevalence and variability in use of physical and chemical restraints in residential aged care facilities; A systematic review and meta-analysis]] |
