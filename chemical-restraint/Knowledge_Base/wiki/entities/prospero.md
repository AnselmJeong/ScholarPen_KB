# PROSPERO

## What It Is
PROSPERO is an international prospective register of systematic reviews in the health and social care domains. It provides a centralized database for registering protocol details of planned systematic reviews, enhancing transparency, reducing duplication, and supporting evidence-based practice.

## Role in the Literature
In [[2019 - Muir-Cochrane et al. - International research into 22 years of use of chemical restraint An evidence overview]]: PROSPERO hosted the protocol registration (ID: CRD 42017055258) ensuring transparency and reducing duplication.
In [[2020 - Bruijn et al. - Physical and Pharmacological Restraints in Hospital Care; Protocol for a Systematic Review]]: PROSPERO served as the international prospective register where the protocol was registered (CRD42019116186).
In [[2020 - Muir-Cochrane et al. - Prevalence of the use of chemical restraint in the management of challenging behaviours associated with adult mental health conditions A]]: PROSPERO functioned as the trial registration system where the systematic review protocol (CRD42017055258) was registered.
In [[2022 - Butterworth et al. - Patients’ and staff members’ experiences of restrictive practices in acute mental health in-patient settings; systematic review and thematic synthesis]]: PROSPERO was used for pre-registration of the review protocol (CRD42020176859), ensuring transparency and reducing duplication.
In [[2024 - Castro et al. - Effectiveness and Safety of Intravenous Medications for the Management of Acute Disturbance (Agitation and Other Escalating Behaviors); A Systematic Review of Prospective Interventional Studies]]: PROSPERO was used for protocol registration (ID: CRD42020216456).
