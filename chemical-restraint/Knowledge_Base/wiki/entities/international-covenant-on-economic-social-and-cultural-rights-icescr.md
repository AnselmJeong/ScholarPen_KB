# International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (ICESCR)

## What It Is
The International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (ICESCR) is a United Nations treaty that obligates states to progressively realize economic, social, and cultural rights, including health and an adequate standard of living. It is a foundational instrument in international human rights law, establishing legally binding commitments that underpin state obligations in areas such as mental health and social welfare.

## Role in the Literature
In [[2005 - Hunt - Report of the Special Rapporteur on the Right of Everyone to the Enjoyment of the Highest Attainable Standard of Physical and Mental Health, Paul Hunt]]: Cited as the legally binding basis for the right to mental health, underpinning state obligations to respect, protect, and fulfill this right through progressive realization.
In [[2019 - Raveesh et al. - How right is right-based mental health law]]: Cited as one of the core international human rights treaties that, along with others, informs the context of rights protections relevant to mental health.
In [[Mental Capacity, Dignity and the Power of International Human Rights -03]]: Described as the counterpart to ICCPR, focusing on socio-economic rights.
In [[Mental Capacity, Dignity and the Power of International Human Rights -05]]: Cited similarly to ICCPR for its recognition of inherent dignity in human rights frameworks.
