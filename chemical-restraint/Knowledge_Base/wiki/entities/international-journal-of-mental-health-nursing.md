# International Journal of Mental Health Nursing

## What It Is
The International Journal of Mental Health Nursing is a peer-reviewed academic journal focused on advancing knowledge, practice, and policy in mental health nursing. It serves as a key platform for disseminating research on clinical interventions, ethical considerations, and systemic issues in mental health care, including the use of coercive practices.

## Role in the Literature
In [[2018 - Cusack et al. - An integrative review exploring the physical and psychological harm inherent in using restraint in mental health inpatient settings]]: Published as the venue for an integrative review examining harm associated with restraint use in inpatient mental health settings.  
In [[2020 - Muir-Cochrane - A wicked problem Chemical restraint towards a definition]]: Served as the publication venue for an editorial defining and critiquing chemical restraint as a complex, persistent challenge in mental health care.  
In [[The Use of Coercive Measures in Forensic Psychiatric Care - 16]]: Cited as a representative forum for research on mental health nursing practices, particularly studies analyzing coercive measures in forensic psychiatric contexts (e.g., Laiho et al. 2014; Dickens et al. 2013).
