# MEMORANDUM

## What It Is
MEDLINE is a comprehensive biomedical literature database maintained by the U.S. National Library of Medicine, indexing peer-reviewed journals in medicine, nursing, dentistry, veterinary medicine, and preclinical sciences. It is a foundational resource for clinical and biomedical research, widely used to identify evidence-based studies due to its rigorous indexing and extensive coverage of high-impact journals.

## Role in the Literature
- In [[2018 - Cusack et al. - An integrative review exploring the physical and psychological harm inherent in using restraint in mental health inpatient settings]]: One of five databases searched for relevant literature.
- In [[2024 - Bennetts et al. - Elimination of restrictive practices from acute adult mental health care services; A qualitative evidence synthesis of the lived experience literature.]]: A core biomedical database searched to capture relevant clinical and psychological studies on restraint and seclusion.
- In [[2024 - Castro et al. - Effectiveness and Safety of Intravenous Medications for the Management of Acute Disturbance (Agitation and Other Escalating Behaviors); A Systematic Review of Prospective Interventional Studies]]: Central; one of the databases searched for eligible studies.
