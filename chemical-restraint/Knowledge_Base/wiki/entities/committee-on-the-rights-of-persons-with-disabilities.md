# Committee on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities

## What It Is
The Committee on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities is a UN expert body responsible for monitoring state implementation of the Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD). It interprets the CRPD’s provisions, issues general comments, and reviews country reports to promote compliance with disability rights standards, particularly in areas such as legal capacity and mental health treatment.

## Role in the Literature
In [[2005 - Hunt - Report of the Special Rapporteur on the Right of Everyone to the Enjoyment of the Highest Attainable Standard of Physical and Mental Health, Paul Hunt]]: Interpreted the CRPD, especially regarding legal capacity and the absolute prohibition of involuntary treatment, offering authoritative guidance on state compliance.
In [[2019 - Scholten et al. - Psychiatric Advance Directives Under the Convention on the Rights of Persons With Disabilities Why Advance Instructions Should Be Able to]]: The UN body whose authoritative interpretation of the CRPD is analyzed as promoting the radical model that undermines PADs.
In [[Coercion and Violence in Mental Health Settings - 09]]: UN body that reviews state implementation of the CRPD and has issued critiques of Germany and Switzerland.
