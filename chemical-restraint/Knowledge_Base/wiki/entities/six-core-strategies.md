# Six Core Strategies

## What It Is
The Six Core Strategies is a framework designed to reduce the use of seclusion and restraint in mental health settings. It emphasizes leadership, data use, workforce development, prevention tools, service user involvement, and debriefing to promote safer, more therapeutic environments. Its application supports systemic change by addressing both organizational practices and relational dynamics in inpatient care.

## Role in the Literature
In [[2018 - Cusack et al. - An integrative review exploring the physical and psychological harm inherent in using restraint in mental health inpatient settings]]: Referenced as a foundational framework linked to Safewards, informing practice implications for restraint reduction.  
In [[2020 - Rodrigues - Implementing Alternatives to Coercion; A Key Component of Improving Mental Health Care]]: Described as a structured approach guiding implementation through leadership, data use, workforce development, prevention tools, service user roles, and debriefing.  
In [[2024 - Savage et al. - Comparison of coercive practices in worldwide mental healthcare; overcoming difficulties resulting from variations in monitoring strategies]]: Cited as an evidence-based framework supporting debriefing, leadership involvement, and data-driven change to reduce coercion globally.  
In [[Coercion and Violence in Mental Health Settings - 12]]: Identified as one of three models helping institutions enhance relational safety by targeting leadership, data, workforce, prevention, patient roles, and debriefing.
