# National Institute for Health and Care Excellence (NICE)

## What It Is
The National Institute for Health and Care Excellence (NICE) is a UK public body that provides national guidance and advice to improve health and social care. It develops evidence-based guidelines for clinical practice, including the management of violence and aggression in mental health settings, which are widely referenced in research on restrictive interventions and coercion.

## Role in the Literature
In [[2020 - Muir-Cochrane - A wicked problem Chemical restraint towards a definition]]: cited for its definition of rapid tranquilization and guidance on managing disturbed/violent behavior  
In [[2022 - O'Donovan et al. - Current trends in restrictive interventions in psychiatry; a European perspective]]: cited for UK guidelines on managing violence and aggression, which inform restraint reduction strategies in NHS settings  
In [[Coercion and Violence in Mental Health Settings - 02]]: referenced for its guidelines on managing violence and therapeutic approaches, which the authors note include relevant principles for preventing patient harm  
In [[Coercion and Violence in Mental Health Settings - 05]]: developer of UK guideline NG10 on violence and aggression management, whose definitions of restrictive interventions (e.g., implied lack of consent for forced medication) contrast with German approach  
In [[The Use of Coercive Measures in Forensic Psychiatric Care - 20]]: referenced as the developer of NG10 guidelines on violence and aggression management, which the author engages with professionally
