# Perceived Coercion Scale (PCS)

## What It Is
The Perceived Coercion Scale (PCS) is a 5-item self-report tool designed to measure subjective feelings of coercion in psychiatric care, adapted from the MacArthur Admission Experience Survey for Korean cultural contexts. It assesses patients’ perceptions of whether their hospitalization aligned with their own will, using a 4-point Likert scale, and is widely used to evaluate the subjective experience of coercion in mental health treatment.

## Role in the Literature
