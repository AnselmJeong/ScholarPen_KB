# Brøset Violence Checklist (BVC)

## What It Is
The Brøset Violence Checklist (BVC) is a six-item dichotomous screening tool designed to assess imminent aggression risk in mental health and correctional settings. It evaluates confusion, irritability, boisterousness, physical threats, verbal threats, and object attack, and is used for per-shift or regular interval monitoring to guide clinical decisions and reduce violence.

## Role in the Literature
- In [[2020 - Dickens et al. - Structured assessments for imminent aggression in mental health and correctional settings Systematic review and meta-analysis]]: The BVC is described as a validated six-item tool used to predict imminent aggression in clinical and correctional environments, supporting structured risk assessment.
- In [[2022 - Burk et al. - Use of a Novel Standardized Administration Protocol Reduces Agitation Pro Re Nata (PRN) Medication Requirements The Birmingham Agitation Management (BAM)]]: The BVC is utilized as a standardized 6-item checklist administered every 4 hours to assess agitation severity and inform PRN medication decisions within the BAM protocol.
- In [[Coercion and Violence in Mental Health Settings - 11]]: The BVC is employed to measure violence risk in psychiatric patients, particularly in studies comparing aggression levels between psychiatric intensive care units (PICUs) and general wards.
