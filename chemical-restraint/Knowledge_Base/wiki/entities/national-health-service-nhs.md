# National Health Service (NHS)

## What It Is
The National Health Service (NHS) is the publicly funded healthcare system of the United Kingdom, providing universal medical services to residents. It operates as a central state institution responsible for delivering health and social care, with its structure and accountability shaped by legislation such as the Human Rights Act 1998.

## Role in the Literature
- In [[2008 - Lepping - Is psychiatry torn in different ethical directions]]: Referenced as the UK public health system whose relationship with citizens is mediated by the Human Rights Act 1998, illustrating legal and ethical frameworks governing psychiatric practice.
- In [[Coercion and Violence in Mental Health Settings - 02]]: Described as the healthcare system where documented incidents of coercion and violence occurred, providing the institutional context for mental health service delivery in the UK.
