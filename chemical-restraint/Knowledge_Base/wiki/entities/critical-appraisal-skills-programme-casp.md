# Critical Appraisal Skills Programme (CASP)

## What It Is
The Critical Appraisal Skills Programme (CASP) is a tool designed to help users critically appraise research evidence, particularly in healthcare and social sciences. It provides structured checklists for assessing the quality, validity, and relevance of studies, supporting evidence-based practice by enabling systematic evaluation of methodological rigor.

## Role in the Literature
- In [[2018 - Cusack et al. - An integrative review exploring the physical and psychological harm inherent in using restraint in mental health inpatient settings]]: Used for appraising study quality across included papers.
- In [[2022 - Butterworth et al. - Patients’ and staff members’ experiences of restrictive practices in acute mental health in-patient settings; systematic review and thematic synthesis]]: Used to assess the methodological quality of included qualitative studies, ensuring only sufficiently rigorous evidence contributed to the synthesis.
