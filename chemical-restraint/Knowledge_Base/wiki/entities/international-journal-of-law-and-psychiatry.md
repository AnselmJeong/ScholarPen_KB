# International Journal of Law and Psychiatry

## What It Is
The International Journal of Law and Psychiatry is a peer-reviewed academic journal focusing on the intersection of law, ethics, and mental health. It serves as a key forum for research on topics such as mental health law, forensic psychiatry, and coercion in psychiatric care.

## Role in the Literature
In [[2021 - Morandi et al. - Mental health professionals feelings and attitudes towards coercion]]: The journal published this study, providing a venue for dissemination of findings on professionals' attitudes toward coercion in mental health settings.
In [[2024 - Birkeland et al. - Abolition of coercion in mental health services - A European survey of feasibility]]: The journal served as the publication outlet for this European survey examining the feasibility of abolishing coercion in mental health services.
