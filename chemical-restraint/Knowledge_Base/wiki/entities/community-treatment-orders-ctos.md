# Community Treatment Orders (CTOs)

## What It Is
Community Treatment Orders (CTOs) are legal mechanisms used to mandate outpatient treatment for individuals with mental illness, allowing involuntary care in community settings rather than hospitalization. They are employed as a tool for managing severe mental health conditions while attempting to balance individual rights with public safety concerns.

## Role in the Literature
- In [[2014 - Mfoafo-M'Carthy et al. - Human Rights Violations and Mental Illness]]: Discussed as a Western intervention representing mandated outpatient treatment that raises human rights concerns.
- In [[2022 - Steinert et al. - Law and psychiatry--current and future perspectives]]: Described as a widely adopted compulsory outpatient intervention with variable implementation and limited evidence of effectiveness.
