# Psychiatric Advance Directives (PADs)

## What It Is
Psychiatric Advance Directives (PADs) are legal documents that enable individuals with mental health conditions to articulate their treatment preferences during periods of competence, to guide care during future crises. They operate within the domain of mental health law and patient autonomy, aiming to uphold self-determination and reduce reliance on coercive interventions.

## Role in the Literature
In [[2019 - Scholten et al. - Psychiatric Advance Directives Under the Convention on the Rights of Persons With Disabilities Why Advance Instructions Should Be Able to]: PADs are evaluated for their effectiveness in aligning mental health practices with the UN Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities, emphasizing the right to autonomy in treatment decisions.
In [[Coercion and Violence in Mental Health Settings - 10]: PADs are shown to reduce involuntary admissions and coercive measures by allowing patients to pre-specify acceptable treatments during competent periods.
