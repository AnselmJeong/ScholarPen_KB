# Joint Commission on Accreditation of Healthcare Organization (JCAHO)

## What It Is
The Joint Commission on Accreditation of Healthcare Organization (JCAHO) is a U.S.-based nonprofit organization that accredits and certifies healthcare organizations and programs. It sets standards for quality and safety in healthcare, influencing clinical practices across hospitals and psychiatric facilities through its accreditation requirements.

## Role in the Literature
- In [[2003 - Currier - The Controversy over “Chemical Restraint” in Acute Care Psychiatry]]: JCAHO's accreditation standards are cited as influencing hospital practices regarding the use of chemical restraints in acute psychiatry.
- In [[2017 - Negroni - On the concept of restraint in psychiatry]]: JCAHO is referenced for its role in shaping definitional and regulatory frameworks around restraint use in psychiatric care.
