# Joint Commission

## What It Is
The Joint Commission is a nonprofit organization that accredits and certifies healthcare organizations and programs in the United States. It establishes evidence-based standards for patient safety and quality of care, playing a critical role in shaping clinical practices across healthcare settings.

## Role in the Literature
In [[2016 - Cunha et al. - Chemical and Physical Restraint of Patients]]: Referenced for its evidence-based guidelines on restraint use, including time-limited orders and mandatory reevaluation protocols  
In [[Coercion and Violence in Mental Health Settings - 12]]: Referenced as an organization that audits procedural safety and security at a macro-level in healthcare institutions.
