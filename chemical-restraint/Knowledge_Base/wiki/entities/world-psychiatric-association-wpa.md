# World Psychiatric Association (WPA)

## What It Is
The World Psychiatric Association (WPA) is the international professional organization for psychiatry, representing national psychiatric associations worldwide and setting global standards for ethical and clinical practice in mental health. It plays a central role in shaping international discourse on psychiatric ethics, human rights, and clinical guidelines.

## Role in the Literature
- In [[2020 - Rodrigues - Implementing Alternatives to Coercion; A Key Component of Improving Mental Health Care]]: Issued the position statement and leads advocacy for reducing coercion in mental health care globally.
- In [[2021 - Appelbaum, Tyano - The WPA Code of Ethics for Psychiatry]]: The international organization that developed and adopted the Code of Ethics for Psychiatry as its official ethical statement.
- In [[2023 - Oute et al. - Conflict and antagonism within global psychiatry A discourse analysis of organisational responses to the UN reports on rights-based approaches in]]: One of the most frequent respondents; represented psychiatric authority in multiple responses.
- In [[2023 - Samochowiec et al. - Ethical dilemmas in contemporary psychiatry; Findings from a survey of National Psychiatric Associations in Europe]]: The international body whose Declaration of Madrid was among the most frequently adopted international ethical codes by participating NPAs.
- In [[2024 - Galderisi et al. - Ethical challenges in contemporary psychiatry an overview and an appraisal of possible strategies and research needs]]: Developed the Code of Ethics for Psychiatry and Declaration of Helsinki; referenced throughout as the primary ethical guideline authority for psychiatry.
- In [[Coercion and Violence in Mental Health Settings - 09]]: Professional organization that has engaged with UN critiques but maintains positions allowing for coercion in certain circumstances.
- In [[Coercive Treatment in Psychiatry - 03]]: Organizer of the psychiatry for the person initiative and host of the 2007 Dresden Conference on coercion and cooperation.
