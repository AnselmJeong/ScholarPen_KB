# American Psychiatric Association

## What It Is
The American Psychiatric Association (APA) is a professional organization representing psychiatrists in the United States, responsible for developing and publishing the Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders (DSM). It plays a central role in shaping psychiatric diagnosis, clinical practice, and policy, and is frequently referenced in discussions about mental health ethics, coercion, and industry influence.

## Role in the Literature
- In [[2012 - Bonnie - Advance Directives A Tool for Reducing Coercion]]: Publisher of *Psychiatric Services*, providing the professional and institutional context for the article’s dissemination to mental health practitioners.
- In [[2020 - Cosgrove, shaughnessy - Mental Health as a Basic Human Right and the Interference of Commercialized Science]]: identified as the developer of the DSM and criticized for its members’ extensive financial ties to pharmaceutical companies, raising concerns about undue industry influence on psychiatric taxonomy.
- In [[Coercion and Violence in Mental Health Settings - 09]]: Professional organization involved in exchanges with UN rapporteurs regarding torture and coercion in psychiatric settings
- In [[Psychiatry and the Law - 07]]: peripheral entity referenced in ethics complaint processes for improper involuntary treatment
