# PRISMA

## What It Is
PRISMA (Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses) is an evidence-based minimum set of items for reporting in systematic reviews and meta-analyses. It standardizes the reporting process to enhance transparency, reproducibility, and methodological rigor in research synthesis.

## Role in the Literature
- In [[2022 - Butterworth et al. - Patients’ and staff members’ experiences of restrictive practices in acute mental health in-patient settings; systematic review and thematic synthesis]]: Framework followed for transparent reporting of the systematic review process, enhancing reproducibility and minimizing bias in study selection and synthesis.
- In [[2024 - Parsaei et al. - Machine Learning for prediction of violent behaviors in schizophrenia spectrum disorders a systematic review]]: The reporting standard adhered to for ensuring transparency in the systematic review process.
