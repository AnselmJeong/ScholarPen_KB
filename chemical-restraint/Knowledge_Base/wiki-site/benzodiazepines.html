Why It Matters

  • Central to debates on acute agitation management due to widespread clinical use despite limited efficacy evidence
  • Highlights tension between common practice and guideline recommendations for first-line treatment
  • Associated with higher risks and reduced effectiveness compared to antipsychotics in psychiatric emergencies
  • Illustrates gaps in protocols for sequential medication use after initial treatment failure
