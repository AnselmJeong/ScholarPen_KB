What It Is

The International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights (ICCPR) is a multilateral treaty adopted by the United Nations in 1966, forming one of the two core international human rights covenants alongside the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights. It establishes legally binding obligations for states to respect and ensure civil and political rights, including the right to life, freedom of expression, and liberty and security of person. In the cited literature, it serves as the foundational legal framework for interpreting human rights protections in mental health and capacity-related contexts, particularly through its provisions on dignity, non-discrimination, and due process.

Role in the Literature

In 2019 - Martin, Gurbai - Surveying the Geneva impasse; Coercive care and human rights: The treaty provides the legal basis for the Human Rights Committee’s proportionality analysis under Article 9 regarding liberty and security of person in coercive care settings. In 2019 - Raveesh et al. - How right is right-based mental health law: It is cited as part of the international human rights framework supporting legal claims for rights-based approaches in mental health legislation. In Mental Capacity, Dignity and the Power of International Human Rights -03: Identified as one of the two treaties that transformed the Universal Declaration of Human Rights into binding international law. In Mental Capacity, Dignity and the Power of International Human Rights -05: Referenced for its incorporation of the UDHR’s principle of inherent dignity into enforceable human rights norms.
