Why It Matters

  • Central to discussions of restrictive practices in psychiatric care
  • Highlights ethical tensions between safety and autonomy in mental health settings
  • Represents a key form of coercion requiring oversight and reform
  • Linked to broader human rights and legal debates on involuntary treatment
