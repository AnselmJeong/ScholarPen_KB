Why It Matters

  • The recovery model is a foundational concept in mental health advocacy and policy reform.
  • It challenges coercive frameworks by emphasizing person-centered, rights-based approaches.
  • It aligns with human rights instruments like the CRPD and ethical principles of autonomy and dignity.
  • It informs alternatives to coercive practices in psychiatric care.
