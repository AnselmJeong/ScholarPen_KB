What It Is

The United Nations Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (UNCRPD) is an international human rights treaty adopted by the UN in 2006 to protect the rights and dignity of persons with disabilities. It establishes a comprehensive framework for civil, political, economic, social, and cultural rights, and is a foundational reference in global disability and mental health rights advocacy.

Role in the Literature

  • In 2014 - Mfoafo-M’Carthy et al. - Human Rights Violations and Mental Illness: discussed as a key legislative framework for understanding mental health rights within international human rights law.
  • In 2019 - Raveesh et al. - How right is right-based mental health law: used as the central international human rights treaty to benchmark the alignment and adequacy of India’s Mental Healthcare Act 2017.
  • In Coercion and Violence in Mental Health Settings - 03: referenced throughout as the foundational international human rights framework for rights-based approaches to mental health care.
