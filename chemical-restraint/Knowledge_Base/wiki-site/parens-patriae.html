Why It Matters

  • Underpins legal authority to make decisions for vulnerable individuals who lack decision-making capacity
  • Central to debates on involuntary treatment and civil commitment in psychiatry
  • Balances state protection with individual rights, especially in cases of mental incapacity
  • Shapes ethical and legal frameworks for surrogate decision-making in medical contexts
