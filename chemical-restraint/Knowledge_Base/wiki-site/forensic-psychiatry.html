Why It Matters

  • Central to understanding the intersection of mental health, law, and public safety
  • Highlights ethical tensions in balancing patient rights with societal protection
  • Informs clinical and legal practices regarding coercion, risk assessment, and treatment
  • Reflects evolving societal values, particularly around autonomy and human rights

Key Positions

Position Core claim Key sources
Risk assessment tools have predictive value The Brøset Violence Checklist and Dynamic Appraisal of Situational Aggression show large effect sizes for predicting imminent aggression 2020 - Dickens et al. - Structured assessments for imminent aggression in mental health and correctional settings Systematic review and meta-analysis
Law and psychiatry influence each other bidirectionally The relationship is shaped by evolving societal values, especially patient autonomy, not legal dominance 2022 - Steinert et al. - Law and psychiatry–current and future perspectives
Psychiatrists have a duty to warn The Tarasoff decision establishes a legal duty to protect identifiable third parties from foreseeable violence Clinical Manual of Psychiatry and Law - 09
Coercion is inherent but constrained Coercion is an inherent element of psychiatry, legitimized only when balanced against rights and safety via legal/ethical limits Coercive Treatment in Psychiatry - 09
Abolishing coercion creates systemic dilemmas UN CRPD-driven abolition poses challenges in forensic, suicide, and dementia care requiring broader reforms Human Rights in Psychiatry - 17
Coercive measures require safeguards Involuntary treatment must meet proportionality, necessity, and procedural safeguards under international human rights law The Use of Coercive Measures in Forensic Psychiatric Care - 03
Coercive practices vary globally No universally least restrictive option; banning one method often increases use of others and causes distress The Use of Coercive Measures in Forensic Psychiatric Care - 07
Staff attitudes shape coercion use Perceived efficacy and views of aggression as intentional drive use, not objective safety or dignity The Use of Coercive Measures in Forensic Psychiatric Care - 16
Staff experience role conflict Workers in high-security settings face conflict between care and security, leading to emotional detachment The Use of Coercive Measures in Forensic Psychiatric Care - 17
