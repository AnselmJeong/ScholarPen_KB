Why It Matters

  • Central to ethical decision-making when patients lack capacity
  • Directly informs substituted consent processes in psychiatric care
  • Balances autonomy protection with beneficent intervention
  • Legally recognized in capacity assessment frameworks

Position Core claim Key sources
Ethical priority Individual preferences must override rigid intervention hierarchies in restraint decisions 2024 - Hempeler et al. - Preferences of Individual Mental Health Service Users Are Essential in Determining the Least Restrictive Type of Restraint
Legal standard Substituted consent applies when patients lack decision-making capacity, based on functional assessment Clinical Manual of Psychiatry and Law - 04
