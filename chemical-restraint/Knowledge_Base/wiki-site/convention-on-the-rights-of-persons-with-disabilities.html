Why It Matters

  • Centralizes legal capacity and decision-making rights in disability rights discourse
  • Challenges biomedical models of mental health by centering human rights and autonomy
  • Sparks debate over compatibility of involuntary treatment with international human rights law
  • Drives global shifts toward supported decision-making and coercion reduction in psychiatry
