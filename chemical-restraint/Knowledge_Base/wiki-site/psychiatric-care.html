Why It Matters

  • Central to understanding the evolution of coercive practices in mental health treatment
  • Highlights the tension between clinical necessity and human rights protections
  • Reflects broader ethical and legal shifts in psychiatric governance and patient autonomy
  • Serves as a focal point for debates on reform, regulation, and alternatives to restraint

Key Positions

Position Core claim Key sources
Historical shift in physical restraint Physical restraint has moved from accepted practice to regulated last resort due to advocacy, litigation, and documented harms 2017 - Masters - Physical Restraint A Historical Review and Current Practice
Human rights-based approach reduces coercion Implementing human rights frameworks lowers coercive measures while empowering patients and staff and shaping policy toward zero coercion 2020 - Broberg et al. - A Human Rights-Based Approach to Psychiatry; Is It Possible
Need for operational definition of chemical restraint A structured definition based on intent, process, and structure is essential for measuring and assessing chemical restraint quality 2024 - Hupe et al. - Defining chemical restraint A preliminary step towards measurement and quality assessment
