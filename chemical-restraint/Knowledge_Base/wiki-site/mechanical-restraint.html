  1. Mechanical restraint

  2. Why It Matters

    • A key coercive practice in psychiatric settings with significant ethical and legal implications
    • Central to debates on reducing coercion in mental health care despite inconsistent definitions
    • Intersects with human rights frameworks and legal standards for patient autonomy and dignity
    • Represents a focal point for reform efforts aimed at minimizing restrictive interventions

  3. Key Positions

    Position Core claim Key sources
    Critical stance Inconsistent definitions and measurement hinder global efforts to reduce coercion in mental health Coercion and Violence in Mental Health Settings - 05
    Reform-oriented Novel approaches are needed to replace or reduce reliance on mechanical restraint 2024 - Steinert et al. - Novel Approaches Needed An Experimental Study with an Alternative to Mechanical Restraint
