Why It Matters

  • Central to ethical and legal debates in mental health care due to its impact on human rights
  • Inconsistent definitions hinder global efforts to reduce coercive practices
  • Core to discussions on autonomy, consent, and patient safety in psychiatric settings
  • Linked to systemic issues like institutional betrayal and racial disparities in care

Open questions

  • How can coercion be consistently defined and measured across diverse mental health systems and cultural contexts?
  • What are the most effective, ethically acceptable alternatives to coercive interventions in acute psychiatric crises?
  • To what extent do structural factors (e.g., racism, resource gaps) drive disparities in the use of coercion?
  • How can supported decision-making models be scaled to reduce reliance on coercive practices while ensuring safety?
