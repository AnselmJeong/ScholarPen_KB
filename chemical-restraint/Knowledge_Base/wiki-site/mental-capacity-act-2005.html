What It Is

The Mental Capacity Act 2005 is a UK law that provides a legal framework for decision-making on behalf of individuals who lack the mental capacity to make specific decisions for themselves. It establishes principles for assessing capacity, making best-interest decisions, and safeguarding the rights of vulnerable adults in health and social care contexts. It is central to ethical and legal discussions about autonomy and paternalism in clinical practice.

Role in the Literature

  • In 2016 - Lepping et al. - Paternalismivi. autonomy - are we barking up the wrong tree: UK law permitting coercion when patients lack capacity, central to the discussion of autonomy vs. paternalism in England and Wales
