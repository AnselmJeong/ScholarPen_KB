Why It Matters

  • Critical safety concern when using antipsychotics for acute agitation management
  • Influences medication selection in psychiatric emergency protocols
  • Directly impacts patient comfort, treatment adherence, and risk of discontinuation
  • Must be weighed against efficacy when evaluating pharmacological interventions for disturbance

Key Positions

Position Core claim Key sources
EPS as a limiting factor in acute agitation treatment Extrapyramidal side effects reduce the tolerability and safety profile of certain antipsychotics used for rapid tranquilization, necessitating careful agent selection 2024 - Castro et al. - Effectiveness and Safety of Intravenous Medications for the Management of Acute Disturbance (Agitation and Other Escalating Behaviors); A Systematic Review of Prospective Interventional Studies
Benzodiazepines as preferred alternative to avoid EPS Midazolam and lorazepam demonstrate efficacy in acute agitation without inducing EPS, making them favorable first-line options in non-elderly populations 2017 - Kousgaard et al. - Effects of Intramuscular Midazolam and Lorazepam on Acute Agitation in Non-Elderly Subjects - A Systematic Review
Need for EPS monitoring in pharmacologic intervention protocols Safety assessments for acute disturbance management must include systematic evaluation of motor side effects to prevent iatrogenic harm 2024 - Castro et al. - Effectiveness and Safety of Intravenous Medications for the Management of Acute Disturbance (Agitation and Other Escalating Behaviors); A Systematic Review of Prospective Interventional Studies
