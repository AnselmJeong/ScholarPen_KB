What It Is

The World Health Organization (WHO) is a specialized agency of the United Nations responsible for international public health. It sets global health standards, provides technical guidance, and leads initiatives on mental health, human rights, and healthcare quality, making it a central authority in shaping policies and practices worldwide.

Role in the Literature

In 2005 - Hunt - Report of the Special Rapporteur on the Right of Everyone to the Enjoyment of the Highest Attainable Standard of Physical and Mental Health, Paul Hunt: cited as a key source of reports, guidelines, and initiatives such as the Mental Health Atlas 2014, MiNDbank, QualityRights initiative, and the World Health Report 2001. In 2014 - Mfoafo-M’Carthy et al. - Human Rights Violations and Mental Illness: cited for global mental health burden estimates, human rights project, and policy frameworks. In 2016 - Cunha et al. - Chemical and Physical Restraint of Patients: cited for its definition of aggressiveness used in the study’s introduction to contextualize behavioral risks. In 2020 - Bruijn et al. - Physical and Pharmacological Restraints in Hospital Care; Protocol for a Systematic Review: referenced in relation to the International Clinical Trials Registry Platform (ICTRP), which will be used to assess outcome reporting bias. In 2020 - Cosgrove, shaughnessy - Mental Health as a Basic Human Right and the Interference of Commercialized Science: cited for its definition of health as encompassing mental well-being and its recognition of mental health as integral to the right to health. In 2020 - Rodrigues - Implementing Alternatives to Coercion; A Key Component of Improving Mental Health Care: developed the Quality Rights Initiative, a key framework cited for reducing coercion through human rights-based training and assessment. In 2024 - Galderisi et al. - Ethical challenges in contemporary psychiatry an overview and an appraisal of possible strategies and research needs: defined telepsychiatry and referenced in discussions of digital psychiatry’s global implications and opportunities. In 2024 - Laureano et al. - Ethical Issues in Clinical Decision-Making about Involuntary Psychiatric Treatment A Scoping Review: referenced for its Involuntary Admission and Treatment checklist used to compare international detention criteria. In 2024 - Savage et al. - Comparison of coercive practices in worldwide mental healthcare; overcoming difficulties resulting from variations in monitoring strategies: provider of the Mental Health Atlas dataset, which currently only tracks involuntary hospital admissions as a proxy for coercion; the study recommends expanding it to include comprehensive restrictive. In Coercion and Violence in Mental Health Settings - 04: cited for promoting recovery-oriented and cooperation-based mental health services. In Coercion and Violence in Mental Health Settings - 05: central to global mental health policy, driving the Quality Rights Initiative that obliges member states to center human rights in service provision and provides the foundational definition of health. In Coercion and Violence in Mental Health Settings - 09: UN agency issuing guidelines that call for avoidance of coercion and respect for human rights in psychiatry. In Coercion and Violence in Mental Health Settings - 10: developer of the QualityRights Initiative to align mental health law and practice with CRPD standards. In Coercive Treatment in Psychiatry - 03: cited for policy influence through resolutions promoting people-centred care and health system responsiveness.
