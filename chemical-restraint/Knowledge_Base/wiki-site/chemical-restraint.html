Why It Matters

  • Central to ethical and legal debates about autonomy, consent, and coercion in psychiatric and aged care settings
  • Frequently overlaps with broader discussions of restraint, forced medication, and human rights protections
  • Subject to inconsistent definitions and measurement across jurisdictions and research
  • Highlighted in international frameworks (e.g., CRPD) as a practice requiring reduction and oversight

Position Core claim Key sources
Clinical effectiveness in acute settings Chemical restraint can rapidly manage acute agitation and aggression, particularly in emergency psychiatry 2019 - Muir‐Cochrane et al. - The effectiveness of chemical restraint in managing acute agitation and aggression; A systematic review of randomized controlled trials
Experiential harms and power dynamics Service users and staff report psychological trauma, loss of dignity, and perceived coercion, even when clinically indicated 2021 - Muir‐Cochrane, Oster - Chemical restraint; A qualitative synthesis review of adult service user and staff experiences in mental health settings
Overuse and polypharmacy risks Routine use in compulsory care settings correlates with high-dose antipsychotics and polypharmacy, raising safety concerns 2021 - Kaikoushi et al. - Prescription patterns in psychiatric compulsory care polypharmacy and high-dose antipsychotics
Systemic critique in aged care Chemical restraint may function as a form of “behavioural euthanasia” in aged care, reflecting systemic neglect and coercion 2023 - Cain et al. - Chemical restraint as behavioural euthanasia; case studies from the Royal Commission into Aged Care Quality and Safety
Conceptual and operational inconsistency Inconsistent definitions and measurement hinder international efforts to reduce coercion despite human rights consensus Coercion and Violence in Mental Health Settings - 05
Philosophical challenge to autonomy Challenges notions of liberty and consent, engaging debates on paternalism, beneficence, and legal capacity 2017 - Negroni - On the concept of restraint in psychiatry
