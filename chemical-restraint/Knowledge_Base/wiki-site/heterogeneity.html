Why It Matters

  • Reveals inconsistencies in measuring and reporting restrictive practices across settings
  • Highlights how geography and methodology influence prevalence estimates
  • Challenges assumptions about uniformity in care practices globally
  • Underscores need for standardized measurement in research and policy

Key Positions

Position Core claim Key sources
Measurement variability drives heterogeneity Differences in assessment tools and definitions explain much of the variation in restraint use 2020 - Lee et al. - Prevalence and variability in use of physical and chemical restraints in residential aged care facilities; A systematic review and meta-analysis
Geographic differences explain heterogeneity Regional variations in policy, culture, and practice account for disparities in restrictive care use 2020 - Lee et al. - Prevalence and variability in use of physical and chemical restraints in residential aged care facilities; A systematic review and meta-analysis, 2024 - Belayneh et al. - Prevalence and variability of restrictive care practice use (physical restraint, seclusion and chemical restraint) in adult mental health inpatient settings
Extreme heterogeneity in mental health settings I² >99% indicates near-total inconsistency in reported restrictive practices, largely due to geography and data methods 2024 - Belayneh et al. - Prevalence and variability of restrictive care practice use (physical restraint, seclusion and chemical restraint) in adult mental health inpatient settings
