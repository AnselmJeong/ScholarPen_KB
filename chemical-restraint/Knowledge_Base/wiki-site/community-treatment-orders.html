Why It Matters

  • Central to debates on balancing public safety and patient autonomy in mental health care
  • Represents a key form of coercive treatment with significant human rights implications
  • Subject to ongoing legal and ethical scrutiny regarding effectiveness and proportionality
  • Reflects tensions between medical authority and legal oversight in psychiatric practice

Key Positions

Position Core claim Key sources
Coercion in psychiatry often lacks therapeutic benefit Psychiatric coercion fails to benefit most patients, is not consistently used as a last resort, and lacks evidence for restoring autonomy Human Rights in Psychiatry - 08
Law and psychiatry relationship is evolving The relationship between law and psychiatry is bidirectional and shaped by evolving societal values, particularly patient autonomy, rather than unidirectional legal dominance 2022 - Steinert et al. - Law and psychiatry–current and future perspectives
Reduction efforts hindered by inconsistency International efforts to reduce coercion are hindered by inconsistent definitions and measurement, despite consensus on core concepts and growing human rights frameworks Coercion and Violence in Mental Health Settings - 05
