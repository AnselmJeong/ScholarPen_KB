What It Is

PubMed is a free, comprehensive biomedical literature database maintained by the National Institutes of Health (NIH). It provides access to millions of citations and abstracts from peer-reviewed journals in medicine, nursing, dentistry, veterinary medicine, and the life sciences. In the cited literature, it serves as a critical source for identifying relevant studies through systematic literature searches.

Role in the Literature

  • In 2014 - Mfoafo-M’Carthy et al. - Human Rights Violations and Mental Illness: used as the database for conducting the literature search on human rights violations and mental illness
  • In 2024 - Parsaei et al. - Machine Learning for prediction of violent behaviors in schizophrenia spectrum disorders a systematic review: one of the primary literature sources searched to identify relevant studies on machine learning applications in schizophrenia spectrum disorders
