Why It Matters

  • Central to understanding coercion in mental health care
  • Directly impacts patient autonomy and human rights
  • Represents a key point of ethical and legal tension in psychiatric treatment
  • Drives ongoing debates about least restrictive alternatives and due process

Key Positions

Position Core claim Key sources
Human rights critique Involuntary hospitalization often violates international human rights standards due to inconsistent application and lack of procedural safeguards Coercion and Violence in Mental Health Settings - 05
Systematic evidence gap Qualitative experiences of legally mandated treatment reveal trauma and distress, highlighting need for patient-centered approaches 2020 - Plahouras et al. - Experiences with legally mandated treatment in patients with schizophrenia A systematic review of qualitative studies
Conceptual ambiguity Inconsistent definitions and measurement of coercion hinder cross-study comparison and policy development despite growing consensus on core principles Coercion and Violence in Mental Health Settings - 05
Treatment justification Coercive treatments, including involuntary hospitalization, are sometimes framed as necessary for safety or beneficence in acute psychiatric crises Coercive Treatment in Psychiatry - 11
