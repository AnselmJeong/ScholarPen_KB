Why It Matters

  • Reveals how commercial interests can distort mental health research and practice
  • Undermines evidence-based approaches by prioritizing industry over social justice
  • Creates ethical tensions in psychiatric care when profit motives influence treatment guidelines
  • Highlights the need for transparency and reform in medical research funding

Position Core claim Key sources
Commercialization critique Industry influence obscures social determinants through biomedical reductionism and shapes research, guidelines, and education to prioritize profit 2020 - Cosgrove, shaughnessy - Mental Health as a Basic Human Right and the Interference of Commercialized Science
