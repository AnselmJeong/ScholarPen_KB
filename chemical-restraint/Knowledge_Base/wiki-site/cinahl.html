What It Is

CINAHL is a comprehensive database of nursing and allied health literature, indexing journals, books, and evidence-based resources in healthcare. It is widely used for systematic and integrative reviews in clinical practice, particularly in mental health and nursing research.

Role in the Literature

In 2018 - Cusack et al. - An integrative review exploring the physical and psychological harm inherent in using restraint in mental health inpatient settings: One of five databases searched for relevant literature. In 2019 - Muir-Cochrane et al. - International research into 22 years of use of chemical restraint An evidence overview: Database searched for nursing and allied health literature relevant to restraint practices in clinical settings. In 2020 - Muir-Cochrane et al. - Prevalence of the use of chemical restraint in the management of challenging behaviours associated with adult mental health conditions A: Nursing and allied health literature database searched for relevant studies. In 2024 - Bennetts et al. - Elimination of restrictive practices from acute adult mental health care services; A qualitative evidence synthesis of the lived experience literature.: One of the primary databases searched for nursing and allied health literature on restrictive practices in mental health settings. In 2024 - Laureano et al. - Ethical Issues in Clinical Decision-Making about Involuntary Psychiatric Treatment A Scoping Review: One of five databases searched for relevant literature.
