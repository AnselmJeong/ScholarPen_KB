Why It Matters

  • Central to reducing coercive practices in mental health care
  • Emphasizes patient autonomy, dignity, and therapeutic relationships
  • Supported as an evidence-based alternative to seclusion and restraint
  • Aligns with human rights frameworks and ethical care standards

Key Positions

Position Core claim Key sources
Trauma-informed care reduces coercion Alternatives to coercion, including trauma-informed care, lower coercive interventions by centering patient autonomy and dignity Coercion and Violence in Mental Health Settings - 18
Leadership role in reduction Psychiatrists should lead efforts to reduce seclusion and restraint via preventive, team-based approaches like the Six Core Strategies 2020 - Masters et al. - The Role of the Psychiatrist in Seclusion and Restraint
Evidence-based alternatives essential Implementing alternatives to coercion is critical for improving care quality and upholding dignity globally 2020 - Rodrigues - Implementing Alternatives to Coercion; A Key Component of Improving Mental Health Care
Shift from routine to last resort Physical restraint has evolved from accepted practice to regulated last resort due to advocacy, litigation, and documented harms 2017 - Masters - Physical Restraint A Historical Review and Current Practice
