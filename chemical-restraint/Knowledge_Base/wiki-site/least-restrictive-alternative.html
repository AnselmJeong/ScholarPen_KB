Why It Matters

  • Guides ethical and legal use of restraint and coercion in psychiatric settings
  • Balances individual autonomy with clinical and safety imperatives
  • Serves as a legal and procedural safeguard in involuntary treatment decisions
  • Reflects evolving standards from social control to rights-based frameworks

Position Core claim Key sources

Legal standard Involuntary hospitalization must comply with least restrictive principles and procedural standards Clinical Handbook of Psychiatry and Law - 02
Rights-based challenge Persons with psychosocial disabilities retain legal capacity and must consent to interventions Coercion and Violence in Mental Health Settings - 09
Ethical tension Coercion remains ethically contested due to conflicts between autonomy and beneficence Human Rights in Psychiatry - 06
Practical limitation No universal best practice standards for coercive treatment exist due to methodological limits Coercive Treatment in Psychiatry - 06
