What It Is

The Mysore Declaration is a regional declaration from the 2013 international symposium in Mysore, India, focused on mental health rights and the elimination of coercive practices in mental health care. It emerged as a foundational reference for rights-based approaches in Indian mental health policy, influencing later legislation such as the Mental Healthcare Act, 2017. The declaration emphasizes dignity, autonomy, and the reduction of discrimination and coercion in mental health settings.

Role in the Literature

  • In 2019 - Raveesh et al. - How right is right-based mental health law: Referenced as a regional declaration that reinforces the rights-based ethos underpinning the Mental Healthcare Act, 2017, particularly in emphasizing the elimination of discrimination and coercion.
  • In Coercion and Violence in Mental Health Settings - 03: Described as the outcome of the 2013 international symposium in Mysore, India, where it defined coercive measures in the Indian context and outlined strategies to minimize coercion in mental health settings.
