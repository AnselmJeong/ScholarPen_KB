Why It Matters

  • Legal reform can fail victims when it misunderstands coercion in law itself
  • Existing frameworks often neglect lived experiences of vulnerable populations
  • Mental health rights are inadequately protected in low-resource and conflict settings
  • Reform requires balancing autonomy with protection needs in complex contexts

Key Positions

Position Core claim Key sources
Critique of coercive control reform Criminalizing coercive control fails victims by misunderstanding law’s coercive nature and ignoring holistic lived experience 2019 - Walklate, Fitz‐Gibbon - The Criminalisation of Coercive Control; The Power of Law
Need for new mental health frameworks International law fails to protect mental health in conflict and low-resource areas; requires new frameworks balancing autonomy and protection 2022 - Miller - Mental health and the law What else is needed for particularly vulnerable contexts facing armed conflict and development obstacles
