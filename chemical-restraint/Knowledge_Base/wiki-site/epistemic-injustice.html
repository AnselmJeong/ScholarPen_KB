Why It Matters

  • Highlights how psychiatric practices can silence or invalidate the knowledge of people with psychosocial disabilities
  • Reveals systemic biases that undermine authentic consent and perpetuate injustice in mental health care
  • Connects to broader human rights frameworks demanding recognition of epistemic agency
  • Underpins critiques of coercive practices by showing how they distort truth-telling and knowledge production
