Why It Matters

  • Central to legal frameworks governing medical decision-making for individuals lacking capacity
  • Directly intersects with ethical principles of autonomy, beneficence, and paternalism in psychiatric care
  • Key mechanism for implementing legally mandated treatment in severe mental illness
  • Raises critical questions about substituted judgment vs. best interests standards

Key Positions

Position Core claim Key sources
Supported decision-making preference Guardianship should be replaced by less restrictive alternatives preserving autonomy 2020 - Plahouras et al. - Experiences with legally mandated treatment in patients with schizophrenia A systematic review of qualitative studies
Necessity of substituted judgment Guardianship remains essential when patients lack decision-making capacity due to severe illness 1980 - Ford - The Psychiatrist’s Double Bind; The Right to Refuse Medication
