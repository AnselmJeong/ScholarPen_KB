What It Is

Safewards is an evidence-based model designed to reduce conflict and containment in psychiatric inpatient settings through staff training, environmental modifications, and relational safety interventions. It promotes alternatives to restrictive practices by enhancing safety and communication between patients and staff. The model matters in mental health literature as a recommended approach to minimizing coercion and improving care quality.

Role in the Literature

In 2018 - Cusack et al. - An integrative review exploring the physical and psychological harm inherent in using restraint in mental health inpatient settings: Model promoted to reduce restrictive practices; cited as a recommended alternative. In 2020 - Muir-Cochrane - A wicked problem Chemical restraint towards a definition: referenced as an evidence-based, least restrictive approach to reducing coercive practices. In 2020 - Rodrigues - Implementing Alternatives to Coercion; A Key Component of Improving Mental Health Care: An evidence-based model designed to reduce conflict and containment in psychiatric wards through staff training and environmental modifications. In Coercion and Violence in Mental Health Settings - 12: presented as another model promoting relational safety through specific interventions like mutual help, clear boundaries, and de-escalation to reduce conflict and containment in mental health wards.
