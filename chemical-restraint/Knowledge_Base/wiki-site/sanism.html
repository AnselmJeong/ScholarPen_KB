Why It Matters

  • Reveals systemic bias in psychiatric practices that undermines the validity of consent for people with psychosocial disabilities
  • Explains how coercive treatments like chemical restraint perpetuate injustice by dismissing lived experience
  • Connects to human rights violations under CRPD and international law regarding autonomy and bodily integrity
  • Central to critiques of psychiatric power structures that prioritize compliance over person-centered care
