Why It Matters

  • A restrictive practice with significant ethical, legal, and clinical implications in psychiatric care
  • Central to debates on patient safety, autonomy, and human rights in acute psychiatric settings
  • Frequently studied in relation to de-escalation and coercive interventions
  • Its reduction is a key indicator of quality improvement in mental health services

Open questions

  • How can de-escalation strategies be optimally implemented and sustained across diverse psychiatric settings to further reduce reliance on physical restraint?
  • What are the long-term psychological and relational impacts of physical restraint on patients, particularly in terms of trust and therapeutic alliance?
  • How do cultural, institutional, and legal contexts shape the acceptability and use of physical restraint as a coercive measure in mental health care?
  • To what extent do alternatives to restraint (e.g., sensory modulation, peer support, advanced directives) achieve comparable safety outcomes without compromising patient autonomy?
