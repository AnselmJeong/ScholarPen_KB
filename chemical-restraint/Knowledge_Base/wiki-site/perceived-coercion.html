Why It Matters

  • Central to understanding patient experience in psychiatric care beyond objective measures
  • Highlights the gap between clinical justification and lived perception of coercion
  • Reveals how therapeutic context can transform coercive experiences from harmful to beneficial
  • Underscores cultural variability in what constitutes coercion and its acceptability

Key Positions

Position Core claim Key sources
Perception vs. justification Coercion may be clinically justified but still experienced as coercive by patients 2012 - Seo et al. - Coercion in psychiatric care Can paternalism justify coercion
Cultural shaping Perception of coercion is influenced by cultural norms around autonomy and family Coercion and Violence in Mental Health Settings - 03
Structural embedding Coercive experience arises from institutional practices, not just specific interventions The Use of Coercive Measures in Forensic Psychiatric Care - 04
Ambivalent experience Coercive measures can be perceived as safe or beneficial when paired with respect and trust The Use of Coercive Measures in Forensic Psychiatric Care - 18
