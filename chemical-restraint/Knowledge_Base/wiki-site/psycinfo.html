What It Is

PsycINFO is a comprehensive bibliographic database covering psychology and related behavioral and social sciences, produced by the American Psychological Association. It indexes scholarly literature including journal articles, books, and dissertations, making it a key resource for mental health research.

Role in the Literature

In 2014 - Mfoafo-M’Carthy et al. - Human Rights Violations and Mental Illness: database used in literature search In 2018 - Cusack et al. - An integrative review exploring the physical and psychological harm inherent in using restraint in mental health inpatient settings: one of five databases searched for relevant literature In 2019 - Muir-Cochrane et al. - International research into 22 years of use of chemical restraint An evidence overview: one of the four databases searched for psychological and behavioral literature on chemical restraint In 2020 - Muir-Cochrane et al. - Prevalence of the use of chemical restraint in the management of challenging behaviours associated with adult mental health conditions A: primary database used for literature search alongside CINAHL, MEDLINE/PubMed, and Google Scholar In 2024 - Laureano et al. - Ethical Issues in Clinical Decision-Making about Involuntary Psychiatric Treatment A Scoping Review: one of five databases searched for relevant literature In 2024 - Parsaei et al. - Machine Learning for prediction of violent behaviors in schizophrenia spectrum disorders a systematic review: a psychology-specific database searched for relevant psychiatric and behavioral studies
