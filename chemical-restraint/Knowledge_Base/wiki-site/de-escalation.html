Why It Matters

  • Reduces physical aggression and restraint use in acute psychiatric settings
  • Addresses high rates of healthcare workplace violence in mental health environments
  • Supports efforts to minimize coercive practices across mental health care pathways
  • Influences ward design and environmental strategies to prevent escalation

Key Positions

Position Core claim Key sources
Effectiveness of training De-escalation training significantly reduces aggression, severe aggression, and physical restraint use 2022 - Celofiga et al. - Effectiveness of De-Escalation in Reducing Aggression and Coercion in Acute Psychiatric Units. A Cluster Randomized Study
Problem context Psychiatric settings have the highest healthcare workplace violence rates, with serious health consequences from exposure Coercion and Violence in Mental Health Settings - 06
Environmental influence Ward design affects aggression and coercive measures; healing environments reduce seclusion and aggression Coercion and Violence in Mental Health Settings - 11
Scope of coercion Coercion extends beyond formal detention to informal and subtle pressures, with rising use despite reduction goals The Use of Coercive Measures in Forensic Psychiatric Care - 08
