Why It Matters

  • Central to informed consent and ethical decision-making in psychiatric treatment
  • Determines whether consent is legally valid and ethically permissible
  • Distinguishes legitimate medical interventions from coercive or unethical practices
  • Critical for protecting vulnerable populations from undue influence in research and treatment contexts

Key Positions

Position Core claim Key sources
Ethical research standards Voluntariness requires ongoing assessment of decisional capacity and voluntarism; ethical research is feasible when prioritized, despite challenges with vulnerable populations Coercive Treatment in Psychiatry - 21
Informed consent framework Voluntariness is a core component of informed consent, alongside competency and information disclosure; competency is assessed functionally, not by diagnosis Clinical Manual of Psychiatry and Law - 04
Determinants of voluntariness Voluntariness depends on whether influences are external, intentional, illegitimate, and causally effective—not merely on the presence of pressure or incentives Coercive Treatment in Psychiatry - 22
