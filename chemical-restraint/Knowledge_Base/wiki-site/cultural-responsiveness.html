Why It Matters

  • Highlights the necessity of integrating cultural context into mental health care to uphold human rights
  • Challenges coercive practices by advocating for voluntary, culturally responsive alternatives
  • Connects patient autonomy with systemic reforms in mental health law and policy
  • Addresses gaps in access to care that respect diverse cultural identities and values
