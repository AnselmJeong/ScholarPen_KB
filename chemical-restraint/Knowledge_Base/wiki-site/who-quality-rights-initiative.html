What It Is

The WHO Quality Rights Initiative is a global program led by the World Health Organization to eliminate coercion and promote human rights in mental health services through training, assessment, and guidance. It aligns clinical practice with international human rights standards, transforming mental health care systems to uphold dignity, autonomy, and legal capacity for service users.

Role in the Literature

In 2020 - Rodrigues - Implementing Alternatives to Coercion; A Key Component of Improving Mental Health Care: Cited as a successfully implemented program in Gujarat, India, providing training and guidance to reduce coercion in mental health care. In Coercion and Violence in Mental Health Settings - 05: Described as a WHO-led framework requiring member states to assess and transform mental health services to align with human rights principles, positioning human rights as core to clinical practice.
