Why It Matters

  • The concept of treatment gap highlights systemic failures in mental health care delivery, particularly the lack of access to effective treatment for severe conditions.
  • It underscores how critiques of psychiatry that ignore treatment gaps may misattribute human rights violations to biomedical approaches rather than systemic under-resourcing.
  • Addressing treatment gaps is essential for evidence-based care and reducing preventable suffering in mental health systems.
  • Recognizing treatment gaps supports arguments for increasing investment in mental health services as a human rights imperative.
