What It Is

The United Nations Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD) is an international human rights treaty that promotes, protects, and ensures the full and equal enjoyment of all human rights by persons with disabilities. It is central to discussions in the literature on mental health law, particularly concerning legal capacity, involuntary treatment, and supported decision-making.

Role in the Literature

  • In [[2019 - Scholten et al. - Psychiatric Advance Directives Under the Convention on the Rights of Persons With Disabilities Why Advance Instructions Should Be Able to]: The international human rights treaty whose interpretation is central to the analysis of PAD effectiveness
  • In [[2024 - Birkeland et al. - Abolition of coercion in mental health services - A European survey of feasibility]: International human rights framework emphasizing autonomy and liberty; cited as a foundation for abolition efforts in mental health
  • In [[2024 - Galderisi et al. - Ethical challenges in contemporary psychiatry an overview and an appraisal of possible strategies and research needs]: Central framework analyzed for implications on psychiatric practice, particularly regarding legal capacity, involuntary treatment, and supported decision-making
  • In [[2024 - Laureano et al. - Ethical Issues in Clinical Decision-Making about Involuntary Psychiatric Treatment A Scoping Review]: Central legal and ethical framework referenced throughout the analysis of involuntary treatment practices
  • In [[Coercion and Violence in Mental Health Settings - 10]: Central framework used to critique involuntary admission as discriminatory and a violation of legal equality
  • In [[Mental Capacity, Dignity and the Power of International Human Rights -01]: The core international human rights treaty analyzed throughout the text, particularly Article 12 on legal capacity
  • In [[Mental Capacity, Dignity and the Power of International Human Rights -06]: The international human rights treaty establishing rights for persons with disabilities, including Article 12 on legal capacity
  • In [[Mental Capacity, Dignity and the Power of International Human Rights -07]: The central international human rights treaty governing disability rights, especially Article 12 on legal capacity
