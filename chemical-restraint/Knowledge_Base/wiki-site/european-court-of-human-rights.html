What It Is

The European Court of Human Rights (ECtHR) is an international court that interprets and applies the European Convention on Human Rights, ensuring compliance by member states. It plays a central role in safeguarding fundamental rights, including protections against inhuman or degrading treatment under Article 3, and its jurisprudence shapes legal standards across Europe, particularly in contexts involving mental health and state obligations.

Role in the Literature

In 2022 - O’Donovan et al. - Current trends in restrictive interventions in psychiatry; a European perspective: The ECtHR’s jurisprudence on Article 3 is cited as a key legal framework shaping interpretations of when psychiatric restraint constitutes prohibited treatment. In Mental Capacity, Dignity and the Power of International Human Rights -03: The ECtHR is referenced as an example of indirect enforcement of socio-economic rights through civil-political mechanisms, illustrated in the case Airey v Ireland.
