Why It Matters

  • Central to legal and ethical frameworks in psychiatry concerning patient autonomy and consent
  • Shapes debates on legal capacity, supported decision-making, and coercive treatment
  • Informs human rights-based approaches to mental health law and policy
  • Drives discourse on the shift from substitute to supported decision-making models

Key Positions

Position Core claim Key sources
UN-CRPD as transformative framework Establishes equal legal capacity and mandates supported decision-making, rejecting substitute decision-making regimes 2017 - Steinert - Ethics of Coercive Treatment and Misuse of Psychiatry, 2022 - Steinert et al. - Law and psychiatry–current and future perspectives
Bidirectional law-psychiatry relationship Law and psychiatry influence each other, with evolving societal values—especially patient autonomy—shaping their interaction 2022 - Steinert et al. - Law and psychiatry–current and future perspectives
