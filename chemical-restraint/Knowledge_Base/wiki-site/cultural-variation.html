Why It Matters

  • Coercive psychiatric practices differ significantly across countries and cultural contexts
  • Universal standards for least restrictive interventions are difficult to establish due to cultural variability
  • Banning one coercive method often leads to increased use of others, reflecting context-dependent adaptation
  • Cultural factors influence perceptions of dignity, autonomy, and acceptable treatment boundaries
