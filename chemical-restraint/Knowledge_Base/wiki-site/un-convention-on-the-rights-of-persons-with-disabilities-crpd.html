What It Is

The UN Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD) is an international human rights treaty adopted in 2006 to promote, protect, and ensure the full and equal enjoyment of all human rights by persons with disabilities. It establishes legal standards for inclusion, accessibility, and non-discrimination, and is central to debates on eliminating coercive practices in mental health care.

Role in the Literature

  • In 2018 - Cusack et al. - An integrative review exploring the physical and psychological harm inherent in using restraint in mental health inpatient settings: Cited regarding the potential unlawfulness of compulsion and coercion under the CRPD’s human rights framework.
  • In 2019 - Martin, Gurbai - Surveying the Geneva impasse; Coercive care and human rights: Interpreted by the CRPD Committee as establishing an absolute right against liberty deprivation based on disability under Article 14.
  • In Human Rights in Psychiatry - 17: Described as the central international treaty driving the abolition of coercion in psychiatry and framing the core legal obligation examined.
