What It Is

AJOB Neuroscience is a peer-reviewed journal focused on the ethical, legal, and social implications of neuroscience research and clinical applications. It serves as a key venue for interdisciplinary discourse at the intersection of neuroscience, bioethics, and clinical practice. Its publications contribute significantly to shaping debates on emerging neurotechnologies and their societal impacts.

Role in the Literature

In 2024 - Birks - Chemical Restraints and the Basic Liberties: Publication venue for the article discussing ethical implications of chemical restraints. In 2024 - Crutchfield, Redinger - Basic Liberties, Consent, and Chemical Restraints: Peer-reviewed journal where the article was published, establishing its relevance to neuroethics and clinical neuroscience discourse. In 2024 - Dougherty et al. - The Lived Realities of Chemical Restraint; Prioritizing Patient Experience: Peer-reviewed journal publishing the commentary that critiques and engages with the arguments presented by Crutchfield and Redinger.
