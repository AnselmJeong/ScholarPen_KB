Why It Matters

  • Central to ethical frameworks in mental health care, emphasizing human rights and disability rights
  • Drives reform toward alternatives to coercion by prioritizing autonomy and legal capacity
  • Provides international legal basis for challenging involuntary treatment and commitment
  • Underpins advocacy for supported decision-making and psychiatric advance directives
