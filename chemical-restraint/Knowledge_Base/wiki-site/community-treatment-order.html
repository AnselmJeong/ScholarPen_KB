Why It Matters

  • Central to debates on coercion in community mental health care
  • Balances treatment access with civil liberties and human rights
  • Frequently discussed alongside involuntary treatment and coercive measures
  • Raises ethical tensions between beneficence and autonomy in recovery-oriented systems
