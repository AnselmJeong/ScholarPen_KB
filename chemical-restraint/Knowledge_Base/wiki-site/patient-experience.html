Why It Matters

  • Shapes ethical and clinical outcomes in psychiatric care
  • Influences treatment adherence and recovery trajectories
  • Reveals systemic issues like stigma and power imbalances
  • Central to human rights and dignity in mental health services

Key Positions

Position Core claim Key sources
Patient experience informs ethical use of coercion Patient perceptions of safety, respect, and therapeutic relationship can mitigate negative experiences of coercive measures 2023 - Brekke et al. - Patients’ experiences with coercive mental health treatment in Flexible Assertive Community Treatment a qualitative study, The Use of Coercive Measures in Forensic Psychiatric Care - 18
Chemical restraint alters subjective experience Psychotropic drugs used in chemical restraint can disrupt authentic consent by altering psychological states, even when decisional capacity appears restored 2024 - Dougherty et al. - The Lived Realities of Chemical Restraint Prioritizing Patient Experience
Coercion and stigma are mutually reinforcing Stigma increases use of coercive measures, and experiencing coercion worsens stigma and public distrust of psychiatry Coercive Treatment in Psychiatry - 04
