What It Is

The American Medical Association (AMA) is a professional organization representing physicians in the United States, established to promote medical science, public health, and the art of medicine. It is widely recognized for developing and maintaining the AMA Code of Medical Ethics, which serves as a foundational guide for ethical conduct in clinical practice. The AMA’s ethical frameworks are frequently referenced in biomedical literature as authoritative sources for understanding physician responsibilities and patient rights.

Role in the Literature

In 2016 - Sidhu, Srinivasraghavan - Ethics and medical practice; Why psychiatry is unique: The AMA’s historical and current medical ethics code is cited as a derivative of early medical ethics frameworks that inform foundational principles in psychiatric practice. In 2022 - Mueller - Ethical and Legal Concerns Associated With Withdrawing Mechanical Circulatory Support; A U.S. Perspective: The AMA Code of Ethics is referenced for its guidance on withdrawing non-beneficial treatment, particularly in the context of mechanical circulatory support decisions.
