What It Is

The European Convention on Human Rights (ECHR) is an international treaty established by the Council of Europe to protect human rights and fundamental freedoms in Europe. It forms the legal foundation for the European Court of Human Rights and is central to judicial interpretations of dignity, liberty, and private life in mental health and capacity contexts.

Role in the Literature

  • In 2018 - Cusack et al. - An integrative review exploring the physical and psychological harm inherent in using restraint in mental health inpatient settings: Cited regarding Articles 3 (inhumane treatment), 5 (liberty), and 8 (private life) in relation to restraint practices.
  • In Mental Capacity, Dignity and the Power of International Human Rights -05: Discussed for its lack of explicit dignity reference but judicial affirmation in cases like Pretty v UK.
