What It Is

The Universal Declaration of Human Rights (UDHR) is a foundational international human rights document adopted by the United Nations in 1948, establishing a common standard of fundamental human rights and freedoms for all people. It serves as the cornerstone of the global human rights framework, underpinning subsequent treaties and national legislation, particularly in the domain of mental health law and ethical practice, by affirming inherent human dignity and equal worth.

Role in the Literature

  • In 2019 - Raveesh et al. - How right is right-based mental health law: Referenced as part of the broader international human rights framework that informs and underpins the UNCRPD and national mental health legislation.
  • In 2024 - Galderisi et al. - Ethical challenges in contemporary psychiatry an overview and an appraisal of possible strategies and research needs: Foundational human rights document referenced as basis for the modern human rights framework applied to mental health contexts.
  • In Mental Capacity, Dignity and the Power of International Human Rights -03: Cited as the foundational document whose rights were divided between ICCPR and ICESCR.
  • In Mental Capacity, Dignity and the Power of International Human Rights -05: Foundational UN document cited for establishing dignity as inherent and linked to equal worth.
