What It Is

The American Psychiatric Association (APA) is a professional organization representing psychiatrists in the United States. It establishes ethical guidelines, clinical standards, and practice policies that shape psychiatric care and professional conduct, serving as a key authority in defining the standard of care and liability expectations in psychiatry.

Role in the Literature

  • In 2016 - Sidhu, Srinivasraghavan - Ethics and medical practice; Why psychiatry is unique: Professional organization that establishes psychiatric ethics guidelines referenced in the paper, particularly regarding confidentiality limits and professional conduct standards.
  • In Clinical Manual of Psychiatry and Law - 05: Develops clinical guidelines (e.g., for tardive dyskinesia management and ECT) cited as authoritative sources for establishing the standard of care in psychiatric practice.
  • In Clinical Manual of Psychiatry and Law - 09: Professional organization referenced for membership statistics used in liability risk estimation; provides context for the rarity of successful litigation against psychiatrists.
