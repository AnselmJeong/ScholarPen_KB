Why It Matters

  • Central to legal capacity and decision-making rights under the UN CRPD
  • Bridges supported and substitute decision-making in capacity assessment
  • Upholds human dignity through indivisibility of civil and socio-economic rights
  • Informs ethical and legal frameworks for mental health and disability rights

Key Positions

Position Core claim Key sources
Supported decision-making as primary Autonomy requires both internal capacities and external support to be realized Mental Capacity, Dignity and the Power of International Human Rights -01, Mental Capacity, Dignity and the Power of International Human Rights -04
Substitute decision-making as last resort Permitted only in ‘hard cases’ when supported decision-making fails, to protect dignity and safety Mental Capacity, Dignity and the Power of International Human Rights -01
Indivisibility of rights Civil-political rights (e.g., autonomy) and socio-economic rights must be upheld together under Article 12 Mental Capacity, Dignity and the Power of International Human Rights -04
