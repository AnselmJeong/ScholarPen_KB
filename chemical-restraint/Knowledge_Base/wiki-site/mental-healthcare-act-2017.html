Why It Matters

  • Promotes rights-based mental healthcare aligned with UNCRPD standards in India
  • Addresses legal capacity and decision-making autonomy for persons with mental illness
  • Highlights tensions between safety interventions (e.g., restraints) and patient rights
  • Links human rights awareness to treatment adherence and emotional well-being

Key Positions

Position Core claim Key sources
Rights-based approach The Act aligns with UNCRPD in advancing a rights-based model but faces conceptual tensions regarding legal capacity 2019 - Raveesh et al. - How right is right-based mental health law
Treatment adherence & emotional needs Medication adherence correlates with human rights awareness; supported admission groups show lower emotional need fulfillment 2021 - Domun et al - Human Rights and Adherence to Treatment in Patients with Severe Mental Illness; Mental Healthcare Act 2017 Perspective
Restraint use & ethics Psychiatric restraints pose risks to autonomy and well-being; should be minimized via legal safeguards and de-escalation 2024 - Paul et al. - Exploring psychiatric patient restraints Balancing safety, ethics, and patient rights in mental healthcare
