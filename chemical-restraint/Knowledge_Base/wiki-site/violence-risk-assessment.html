Why It Matters

  • Critical for preventing harm in clinical settings through early risk identification
  • Integrates legal obligations like Tarasoff duty with clinical assessment practices
  • Requires structured tools to balance static/dynamic factors and environmental context
  • High diagnostic accuracy tools (e.g., AUC 0.88) improve predictive validity over unstructured methods

Key Positions

Position Core claim Key sources
Evidence-based assessment Structured tools like Fordham Risk Screening Tool show strong diagnostic accuracy (AUC 0.88) in ED settings vs. criterion standards 2023 - Racine et al. - Utilization of the Fordham Risk Screening Tool for violence risk assessment in an emergency department
Legal duty context Tarasoff establishes duty to protect identifiable third parties, but liability limited by predictive uncertainty and jurisdictional variation Clinical Manual of Psychiatry and Law - 09
Multifactorial integration Effective assessment requires static/dynamic risk factors, protective factors, and staff/environmental elements via collaborative methods Coercion and Violence in Mental Health Settings - 07
