Why It Matters

  • Measures subjective experience of coercion in psychiatric treatment contexts
  • Distinguishes between objective pressure and perceived voluntariness in clinical and research settings
  • Informs ethical assessment of consent validity under conditions of influence
  • Supports evaluation of coercive practices beyond mere presence of external pressure

Key Positions

Position Core claim Key sources
Perceived coercion depends on specific qualities of influence Voluntariness in research consent requires influences to be external, intentional, illegitimate, and causally effective—not merely the presence of pressure or incentives Coercive Treatment in Psychiatry - 22
Measurement of perceived coercion is essential for ethical evaluation The MacArthur Perceived Coercion Scale captures the subjective dimension of coercion that objective measures miss Coercive Treatment in Psychiatry - 22
