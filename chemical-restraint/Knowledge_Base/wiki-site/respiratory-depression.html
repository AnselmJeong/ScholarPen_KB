Why It Matters

  • It is a serious adverse effect of certain pharmacological interventions used in acute agitation management.
  • Monitoring for respiratory depression is critical to patient safety during rapid tranquilization.
  • It influences risk-benefit assessments when selecting medications for behavioral emergencies.
  • It is a key consideration in avoiding harm and upholding the principle of non-maleficence.

Position Core claim Key sources
Respiratory depression as a risk of benzodiazepines Benzodiazepines used for acute agitation can cause respiratory depression, particularly when administered intravenously or in combination with other CNS depressants 2017 - Kousgaard et al. - Effects of Intramuscular Midazolam and Lorazepam on Acute Agitation in Non-Elderly Subjects - A Systematic Review 2024 - Castro et al. - Effectiveness and Safety of Intravenous Medications for the Management of Acute Disturbance (Agitation and Other Escalating Behaviors); A Systematic Review of Prospective Interventional Studies
Route-dependent risk Intramuscular administration may reduce but not eliminate respiratory depression risk compared to intravenous routes 2017 - Kousgaard et al. - Effects of Intramuscular Midazolam and Lorazepam on Acute Agitation in Non-Elderly Subjects - A Systematic Review 2024 - Castro et al. - Effectiveness and Safety of Intravenous Medications for the Management of Acute Disturbance (Agitation and Other Escalating Behaviors); A Systematic Review of Prospective Interventional Studies
