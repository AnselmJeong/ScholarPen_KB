# Overview

## Current Coverage

- Our values and our historical understanding of psychiatrists: Historical understanding of psychiatry requires contextualizing past practices within their own societal and scientific frameworks rather than judging by present values.
- Blame or discovery Walter Benjamin's iJetztzeit,i Purdue Pharma LP and ‘our values and our historical understanding of psychiatrists’: Ikkos argues that the primary driver of historical inquiry in psychiatry should be discovery of uncomfortable truths, not fear of unfair blame, invoking Benjamin's Jetztzeit.
- Beyond presentism; Memory studies, deep history and the challenges of transmission: Memory studies must move beyond Eurocentric 'presentism' by integrating 'deep history' and shifting from a model of linear replication to one of relational activation in the 'deep present'.
- The Emergence of Psychiatry; 1650–1850: Psychiatry emerged from a contingent convergence of mind, brain, and asylum developments, with an inherent mind-brain dualism constituting its professional identity.
- Psychiatry's need for Vergangenheitsbewältigung; ‘culture wars’, cognitive dissonance and coming to terms with the past: UK psychiatry must engage in Vergangenheitsbewältigung—honest reckoning with its eugenic, racist, and homophobic past—to overcome cognitive dissonance and build a more authentic professional future.
- Introduction; Whose Present Which History: The post-2016 crisis of democracy normalized presentism in the history profession, but this turn is marked by provincial Eurocentrism and no consensus on whose present is of concern.
- Voicing the Ancestors; Readings for the Present from Anthropology's Past: The rigid dichotomy between presentism and historicism is a historical artifact; 'enlightened presentism' allows the recovery of neglected ancestors to nourish current theory.
- Psychopathologies of time; Defining mental illness in early 20th-century psychiatry: Early 20th-century psychiatrists defined mental illness as a breakdown in synthesis between subjective and objective time, using altered future orientation as a key diagnostic criterion.
