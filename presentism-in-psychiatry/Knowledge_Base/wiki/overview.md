## Current Coverage

- Beyond presentism; Memory studies, deep history and the challenges of transmission: Memory studies must move beyond Eurocentric 'presentism' by integrating 'deep history' and shifting from a model of linear replication to one of relational activation in the 'deep present'.
- Voicing the Ancestors; Readings for the Present from Anthropology's Past: The rigid dichotomy between presentism and historicism is a historical artifact; 'enlightened presentism' allows the recovery of neglected ancestors to nourish current theory.
- The new history of psychology; Some (different) answers to Lovett’s five questions.: Brock argues that the 'New History' of psychology represents a fundamental methodological shift toward externalism and critical analysis, countering Lovett's claim that it is a distinction without a difference.
- From Madness to Mental Health - 10: War neurosis manifests differently by rank, with privates prone to suggestible hysteria and officers to anxiety neurosis driven by repression and responsibility.
- 010-chapter-ten: The interwar period saw a shift toward aggressive somatic treatments in psychiatry, driven by a crisis of professional legitimacy and the exploitation of captive patient populations.
- 003-3-a-biological-century: Psychiatry transitioned from utopian 'moral treatment' to a biomedical paradigm that reduced mental illness to organic brain disease, though it continued to rely on common-sense 'without cause' criteria for diagnosis.
