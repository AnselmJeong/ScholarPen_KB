# Association of Medical Superintendents of American Institutions for the Insane

## What It Is
The Association of Medical Superintendents of American Institutions for the Insane (AMSAll) was the first professional organization for asylum superintendents in the United States, founded in 1844 to assert medical authority over the care of the insane. It is significant in the literature as the direct predecessor of the American Psychiatric Association (APA) and as a key institution that shaped early American psychiatry by consolidating institutional control within a medical framework.

## Role in the Literature
- In [[003-3-a-biological-century]]: Described as the body that institutionalized medical control over U.S. asylums in 1844.
- In [[2014 - Hirshbein - History, Memory, and Profession; A View of American Psychiatry Through APA Presidential Addresses, 1883–2003]]: Identified as the predecessor organization to the APA, founded in 1844.
- In [[2022 - Kendler et al. - The Emergence of Psychiatry; 1650–1850]]: Referred to as the precursor organization to the American Psychiatric Association, founded in 1844, with its name highlighting the institutional basis of early psychiatry.
