# National Institute of Mental Health (NIMH)

## What It Is
The National Institute of Mental Health (NIMH) is a federal agency and the lead body for mental health research in the United States. It plays a critical role in shaping psychiatric practice by directing funding and determining the dominant research paradigms. In this literature, it is portrayed as a primary driver of the shift toward biomedical and disease-based models of psychiatry.

## Role in the Literature
- In [[006-6-the-decline-and-fall-of-dynamic-psychiatry]]: A government body that shifted focus away from psychosocial programs (CMHCs) toward biological research.
- In [[007-7-diagnostic-psychiatry]]: The primary funding body that pressured psychiatry to adopt a biomedical, disease-based model.
- In [[009-9-the-successes-and-failures-of-the-dsm-revolution]]: A primary funding body for epidemiological research that eventually disavowed DSM categories in favor of other research methods.
- In [[2023 - George et al. - Cycles of reform in the history of psychosis treatment in the United States]]: The federal lead on mental health research and the developer of the Community Support Program.
- In [[A History of Psychiatry - 09]]: A federal agency that promoted depression awareness and funded field trials for the DSM-III.
