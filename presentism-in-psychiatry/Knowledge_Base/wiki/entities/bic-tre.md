# Bicêtre

## What It Is
Bicêtre is a historic Parisian hospital and hospice that was a key site in the development of early psychiatry. It housed male patients, including many incarcerated as insane, and became the setting where Philippe Pinel and lay governor Jean-Baptiste Pussin pioneered moral treatment principles in the late 18th century. The institution is significant in the history of psychiatry for symbolizing the shift from custodial confinement to more humane therapeutic approaches.

## Role in the Literature
- In [[007-chapter-seven]]: Referenced as the Parisian hospital where Pussin and Pinel developed key moral treatment principles.
- In [[A History of Psychiatry - 02]]: Described as the Parisian hospice for men where Philippe Pinel served from 1793; notes that it housed 245 insane persons in 1788.
- In [[History of Madness - Gary Gutting]]: Mentioned as a French prison/hospital, specifically for the public exhibition of the mad.
