# Bethlem Hospital

## What It Is
Bethlem Hospital, infamously known as "Bedlam," is a historic London asylum and a landmark institution in the history of psychiatry. It serves as a reference point for early psychiatric practice, popular misconceptions, and the evolution of mental health care. Its archives have become a crucial resource for scholarly revision of the institution's role.

## Role in the Literature
- In [[1991 - Scull - Psychiatry and its historians]]: Its archives were exploited for the first time in Jonathan Andrews's thesis, transforming historical understanding of the institution.
- In [[2014 - Houston - A LATENT HISTORIOGRAPHY THE CASE OF PSYCHIATRY IN BRITAIN, 1500–1820]]: It is the subject of popular misconceptions and a site of sustained scholarly attention.
- In [[2022 - Kendler et al. - The Emergence of Psychiatry; 1650–1850]]: It serves as a reference point for early psychiatric practice and the work of physician John Haslam.
