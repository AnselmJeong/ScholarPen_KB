# Royal College of Psychiatrists

## What It Is
The Royal College of Psychiatrists is the principal professional body for psychiatrists in the United Kingdom, setting standards for training, practice, and ethics. In the literature, it serves as the institutional locus for debates on historical accountability, institutional apologies, and the re-evaluation of past figures in psychiatry.

## Role in the Literature
- In [[2022 - Lepping, Poole - Psychiatry's need for Vergangenheitsbewältigung; ‘culture wars’, cognitive dissonance and coming to terms with the past]]: The College is referenced as the central professional body that issued apologies for racism and homophobia, which the authors critique as insufficient, and its celebratory institutional history (*Celebrating Our History*) is identified as problematic.
- In [[2023 - Hilton - Our values and our historical understanding of psychiatrists]]: The College appears as the central institution whose historian-in-residence (the author) provides context for the debate about judging historical figures.
- In [[2023 - Hilton - Sailing between Scylla and Charybdis; invited response to ‘Blame or discovery’]]: The College is Hilton’s institutional home as historian-in-residence; the article appears in the College’s bulletin and reflects its historical interest group.
