# The Anatomy of Melancholy

## What It Is
The Anatomy of Melancholy is a comprehensive 17th-century compendium written by Robert Burton. It serves as a vast synthesis of historical, medical, and philosophical thought regarding depression and melancholy. In this literature, it is referenced as a foundational reflection on the universality and nature of mental distress.

## Role in the Literature
* In [[002-2-before-psychiatry]]: Referenced as a synthesis of 2,000 years of thought on depression.
* In [[004-chapter-four]]: Described as a comprehensive 17th-century synthesis of knowledge on melancholy.
* In [[From Madness to Mental Health - 04]]: Cited as a comprehensive reflection on the universality of melancholy.
