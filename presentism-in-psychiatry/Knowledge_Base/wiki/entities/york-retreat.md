# York Retreat

## What It Is
The York Retreat, founded in 1796 by Quaker William Tuke near York, England, was a pioneering private asylum that became the model for moral treatment—a humane, non-coercive approach to mental illness. Its practices, popularized by Samuel Tuke's 1813 account, shaped psychiatric reform across the English-speaking world and remain a touchstone in the history of psychiatry.
