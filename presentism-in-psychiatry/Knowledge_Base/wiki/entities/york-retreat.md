# York Retreat

## What It Is
The York Retreat is a Quaker-founded private asylum established in 1796 near York, England, that pioneered the moral treatment approach to mental illness by emphasizing kindness, respect, and minimal coercion. It was developed and practiced by William and Samuel Tuke, and its principles influenced asylum reform across the English-speaking world. In the literature, it is presented as a model institution that transformed psychiatric care and is often the subject of micro-historical studies.
