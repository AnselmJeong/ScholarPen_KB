# Journal of the History of the Behavioral Sciences (JHBS)

## What It Is
The Journal of the History of the Behavioral Sciences (JHBS) is a scholarly publication focused on the historical development of psychology, anthropology, and other behavioral disciplines. It serves as a critical venue for methodological debates regarding how the history of science is written and interpreted.

## Role in the Literature
* In [[1965 - Stocking - On the Limits of Presentism and Historicism in the HIstoriography of the Behaviral Sciences]]: The publication venue for George Stocking's influential discussion on the limits of presentism and historicism.
* In [[2019 - Bashkow - On History for the Present; Revisiting George Stocking's Influential Rejection of “Presentism”]]: The venue where the foundational debate over presentism was originally sparked and promulgated.
* In [[2019 - Bashkow et al. - Voicing the Ancestors; Readings for the Present from Anthropology's Past]]: The publication where the early theoretical tensions between presentism and historicism were articulated.
