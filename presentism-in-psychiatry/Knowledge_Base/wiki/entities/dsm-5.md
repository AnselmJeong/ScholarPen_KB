# DSM-5

## What It Is
The DSM-5 (Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders, Fifth Edition) is the primary diagnostic manual used to categorize mental disorders in Western clinical practice. Published in 2013, it is a critical tool for diagnosis, research, and treatment, though it has been criticized for expanding diagnostic criteria and failing to incorporate neuroscientific advances.

## Role in the Literature
- In [[001-1-puzzles-of-mental-illness]]: Referenced as the standard diagnostic manual that defines how mental disorders are categorized in Western practice.
- In [[009-9-the-successes-and-failures-of-the-dsm-revolution]]: Noted for its attempt to introduce dimensional measurement, which ultimately failed, preserving the categorical structure of prior editions.
- In [[010-10-the-past-and-future-of-mental-illness]]: Cited as the current edition, criticized for broadening definitions (e.g., removing the bereavement exclusion) and lacking integration of neuroscientific findings.
