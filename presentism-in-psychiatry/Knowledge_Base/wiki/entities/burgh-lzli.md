# Burghölzli

## What It Is
Burghölzli is the psychiatric university clinic of the University of Zurich, known as the institutional home of Eugen Bleuler’s clinical and theoretical work (1898–1927). It served as a training site for influential figures like Jung, Binswanger, and Brill, and as a hub for psychoanalytically oriented research and therapy. In the literature, it represents a key site where modern psychiatry, psychoanalysis, and early biological treatments converged.

## Role in the Literature
- In [[2011 - Berrios - Eugen Bleuler's Place in the History of Psychiatry]]: Burghölzli is described as the Zurich psychiatric clinic where Bleuler served as director, the institutional base for his clinical and theoretical innovations and the training site for Jung, Binswanger, Brill, Minkowski, and others.
- In [[A History of Psychiatry - 06]]: Burghölzli is referenced as the university psychiatric clinic in Zurich where Jung, Bleuler, and others ran a psychoanalytically oriented seminar that influenced younger psychiatrists.
- In [[A History of Psychiatry - 07]]: Burghölzli is cited as the Zurich university psychiatric clinic where Klaesi developed prolonged sleep therapy with Somnifen.
