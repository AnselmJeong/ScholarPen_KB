# Psychiatry's need for Vergangenheitsbewältigung; ‘culture wars’, cognitive dissonance and coming to terms with the past

## Summary
This editorial explores the moral and professional necessity for UK psychiatry to confront its history of involvement in eugenics, racial hygiene, and imperialist oppression. The authors argue that the profession suffers from a collective amnesia, sustained by a national narrative of British "exceptionalism" and progressivism, which prevents a full reckoning with the "shameful" aspects of its past.

The authors utilize a conceptual framework centered on *Vergangenheitsbewältigung* (coming to terms with the past), contrasting the UK's relative avoidance of its colonial psychiatric legacy with Germany's more concerted efforts to address Nazi-era crimes. They argue that this historical ignorance is not benign but manifests in contemporary practice as structural racism, specifically the disproportionate use of coercion against patients of African heritage.

The piece concludes that true professional integrity can only be achieved by integrating historical shame into the professional identity rather than sequestering it. They call for a broadening of psychiatric curricula to include these failures, arguing that such an honest analysis is the only path toward a more authentic and equitable future for the profession.

## Key Entities
- **Royal College of Psychiatrists** (org) — The professional body in the UK, cited as taking tentative steps toward acknowledging racism but requiring deeper systemic change.
- **University College London (UCL)** (org) — Site of the Eugenics Laboratory and associated with the work of Francis Galton.
- **British Psychological Society** (org) — Cited as an institution still influenced by imperial legacies and institutional racism.
- **Eugenics Laboratory** (org) — A key institution for the development of concepts regarding racial hygiene and genetic worth.

## Key Concepts
- **Vergangenheitsbewältigung** — The process of analyzing and accepting past failures and moral ambiguities to build a better, more authentic future.
- **Cognitive Dissonance** — The psychological tension experienced by psychiatrists when their image of the profession as "progressive" clashes with historical evidence of its role in racism and eugenics.
- **Eugenics / Racial Hygiene** — The historical "science" of improving the human species via selective breeding and the classification of "degenerate" traits, used to justify white supremacy.
- **Culture Wars** — Modern societal conflicts over historical narratives where critical views of the Empire are framed as "anti-British" or non-patriotic.
- **Hereditary Degeneration** — The outdated theory that mental illness is an untreatable genetic flaw, which justified "therapeutic nihilism" and cruel interventions.

## Main Arguments & Findings
- **Core claim**: UK psychiatry's historical ties to eugenics and imperialism directly inform modern structural racism and clinical coercion of minority patients.
  - Evidence: Historical records of Francis Galton’s racial grading and Henry Maudsley’s claims regarding the inferiority of non-white brains; modern meta-analyses showing disproportionate coercion of patients of African heritage.
  - Strength: Strong historical attribution; the causal link to modern practice is interpretive but supported by cited literature.
- **Core claim**: Defensiveness toward critical histories is a result of cognitive dissonance fueled by a "benign" national narrative.
  - Evidence: Comparison between the British "insightless pride" in Empire and the German model of confronting historical atrocities.
  - Strength: Theoretically sound psychological explanation for professional resistance.

- **Professional Pride vs. Historical Truth**: The tension between the psychological need for self-esteem/professional pride and the necessity of acknowledging shameful historical facts.
- **National Narrative vs. Colonial Reality**: The clash between the narrative of Britain as a "civilizing force" and the historical reality of oppression and exploitation.
