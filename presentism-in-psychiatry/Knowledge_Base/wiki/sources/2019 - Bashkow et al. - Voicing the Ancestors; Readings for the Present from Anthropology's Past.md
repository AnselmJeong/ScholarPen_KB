# Voicing the Ancestors; Readings for the Present from Anthropology's Past

## Summary
This work introduces "Voicing the Ancestors," a scholarly genre designed to recover, reinterpret, and share neglected historical texts from the past to inspire contemporary anthropological theory and practice. The project emerged from a series of sessions at the American Anthropological Association (AAA) meetings, where scholars read past texts aloud to expand the discipline's "invisible genealogies."

A significant portion of the text is dedicated to a historiographical re-evaluation of George Stocking's influential 1965 rejection of "presentism." Bashkow argues that Stocking's early stridency was a reaction to the "naive positivism" and "hero worship" prevalent in the early volumes of the *Journal of the History of the Behavioral Sciences (JHBS)*, which sought to use history to prove a linear progress of science. 

The authors demonstrate that Stocking himself spent the subsequent fifty years qualifying his position, moving toward an "enlightened presentism" (a term attributed to Dell Hymes). This approach acknowledges that while the past should be understood in its own context, historical study is most valuable when it brings renewed attention to problems that remain pertinent to the present.

## Key Entities
- **American Anthropological Association (AAA)** (type: org) — The professional body that hosted the "Voicing the Ancestors" sessions.
- **Journal of the History of the Behavioral Sciences (JHBS)** (type: tool) — The publication where the early debate between presentism and historicism was articulated.

## Key Concepts
- **Presentism** — The practice of evaluating historical material based on present-day purposes, goals, or theoretical poles; traditionally viewed as a "methodological vice."
- **Historicism** — The methodological imperative to understand past writers and ideas within the specific context of their own time.
- **Enlightened Presentism** — A nuanced approach where history is used to highlight problems that are still relevant but have fallen out of the current disciplinary agenda.
- **Invisible Genealogies** — The process of rediscovering neglected or underappreciated ancestors in a field to reshape its intellectual foundations.
- **Scientific Paradigm (Kuhnian)** — The view of science as a structured community activity based on tacit assumptions rather than a linear accumulation of knowledge.

## Main Arguments & Findings
- **Core claim**: The dichotomy between presentism and historicism is often used as a "meme" but is functionally limiting.
  - Evidence: Analysis of George Stocking's evolving writings from 1965 to 2010, showing a retreat from strict historicism.
  - Strength: Very strong regarding Stocking's personal intellectual evolution.
- **Core claim**: The original rejection of presentism was a reaction to the misuse of history to legitimize "scientific progress."
  - Evidence: Archival analysis of early *JHBS* editorials and articles that displayed "naive positivism" and "hero worship."
  - Strength: Strong, as it contextualizes a widely cited methodological rule within a specific historical moment.
- **Core claim**: "Voicing the Ancestors" serves as a legitimate tool for disciplinary nourishment.
  - Evidence: Successful implementation of session formats at AAA meetings and the recovery of pertinent texts (e.g., Lévi-Strauss's 1952 work on race).
  - Strength: Moderately strong (largely practitioner-based/anecdotal).

## Contradictions & Tensions
- **Professional Historians vs. Practitioners**: There is a fundamental tension between the historian's goal of understanding the past "for its own sake" and the practitioner's goal of finding tools for current work.
- **The "Presentism" Paradox**: While "avoiding presentism" is often cited as a primary rule of historiography, the authors argue it is practically impossible to avoid all traces of it.

## Open Questions
- How can the "Voicing the Ancestors" genre be formalized into a peer-reviewed academic format without losing its experimental and playful essence?
