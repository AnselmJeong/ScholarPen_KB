# asylum

## Why It Matters
- The asylum is the institutional setting where modern psychiatry was forged, linking medical authority to the management of madness.
- The failure of moral treatment and chronic accumulation of patients led to degeneration theory as a defensive ideology, shaping later eugenic policies.
- Deinstitutionalization did not end the asylum’s function; it transinstitutionalized patients into prisons and homeless shelters.
- The asylum’s physical and administrative structure embodied the mind-brain dualism that became central to psychiatry’s professional identity.
