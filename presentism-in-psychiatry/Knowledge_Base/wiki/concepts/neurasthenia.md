# Neurasthenia

## Why It Matters
- Neurasthenia exemplifies the tension in psychiatry between adopting a biomedical model and relying on common‑sense, non‑organic criteria for diagnosis.
- The concept marks a transitional moment when moral treatment gave way to a reductionist view of mental illness as organic brain disease.
- Understanding neurasthenia helps trace the historical roots of present‑day debates over the classification and causation of mental disorders.
