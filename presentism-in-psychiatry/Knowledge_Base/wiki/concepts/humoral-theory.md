# humoral theory

## Why It Matters
- Humoral theory provided the first systematic naturalistic framework for understanding mental disorder, linking madness to bodily imbalances rather than divine punishment.
- It shaped the diagnostic categories of melancholia and mania, which persisted for centuries and influenced later psychiatric classification.
- Islamic civilization’s preservation of Galenic humoral theory enabled the creation of early hospitals for the insane, contrasting with Christian Europe’s reliance on supernatural explanations.
- The coexistence of humoral naturalism with supernatural causes reveals a fundamental tension in pre-modern psychiatry that still echoes in debates about medical vs. moral/spiritual models.

## Open questions
- How did humoral theory manage to coexist with supernatural explanations for centuries without being fully displaced?
- Was humoral theory truly naturalistic, or did it still embed moral/value judgments about what counted as "disproportionate" behavior?
- Why did Islamic civilization preserve humoral theory institutionally while Christian Europe abandoned it for miracle-based healing?
- The sources do not discuss the eventual decline of humoral theory – what factors led to its replacement?
