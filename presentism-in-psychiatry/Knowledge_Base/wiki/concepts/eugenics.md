# eugenics

## Why It Matters
- Eugenics represents a deeply controversial chapter in psychiatry’s past, often omitted from official professional histories.
- Understanding eugenics requires navigating the tension between presentist moral judgment and historical contextualization.
- The archive reveals how coercive social control (sterilization, segregation) coexisted with genuine therapeutic innovation.
- Honest engagement with this history is necessary for psychiatry to build an authentic professional future.
