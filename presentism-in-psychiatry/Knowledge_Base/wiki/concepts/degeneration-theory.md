# Degeneration Theory

## Why It Matters
* Tracks the shift from psychological/moral interpretations of mental illness to organic, hereditary models.
* Provides context for the adoption of aggressive somatic treatments during the interwar period.
* Links the evolution of psychiatric diagnosis to a biomedical understanding of brain disease.

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Biomedical Shift | Psychiatry transitioned from utopian moral treatment to a paradigm reducing illness to organic brain disease. | [[003-3-a-biological-century]] |
| Somatic Aggression | A crisis of professional legitimacy led to the use of aggressive somatic treatments on captive populations. | [[010-chapter-ten]] |
