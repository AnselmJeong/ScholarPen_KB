# Presentism

## Why It Matters
- Presentism is a central debate in historiography, particularly for the history of psychiatry and the behavioral sciences.
- It forces scholars to decide whether to judge past actors by current moral standards or to understand them in their own context.
- The archive’s 14 sources explore presentism’s limits, ethical implications, and its entanglement with diversity, racism, and transformative justice.

## Open questions
- How can historians balance the ethical imperative to address past injustices with the need to avoid anachronistic judgments? (Hoerger 2023, Arya 2024)
- Is presentism a necessary stance for progressive historical scholarship, or does it inevitably distort understanding? (Valsiner & Brinkmann 2015 vs. Stocking 1965)
- What are the legitimate forms of presentism in the history of science, and how do they relate to historical epistemology? (Loison 2016)
- How does presentism interact with contemporary cultural movements such as “woke” critique? (Gil 2023)
