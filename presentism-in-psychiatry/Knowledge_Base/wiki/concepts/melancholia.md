# melancholia

## Why It Matters
- Melancholia is one of the earliest named categories of mental disorder, appearing in ancient Greek and Hippocratic medicine alongside mania.
- Its definition in antiquity (madness without rational cause or disproportionate to circumstance) established a diagnostic criterion that persisted for centuries.
- The concept illustrates the historical tension between naturalistic (Hippocratic) and supernatural explanations of mental illness.
- Tracing melancholia’s evolution provides historians of psychiatry with clinical data to challenge or refine modern diagnostic classification (e.g., in the DSM system).
