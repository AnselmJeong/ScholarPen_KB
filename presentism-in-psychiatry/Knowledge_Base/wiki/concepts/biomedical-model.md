# Biomedical model

## Why It Matters
- The biomedical model is the dominant framework in modern psychiatry, shaping diagnosis, treatment, and research.
- It underpins the rise of diagnostic systems like DSM-III, which structured mental disorders as discrete biological entities.
- Its ascendancy is historically linked to the decline of dynamic psychiatry, marking a key shift in psychiatric theory.
- The model remains a focal point of critique from anti-psychiatry and medicalization perspectives.
