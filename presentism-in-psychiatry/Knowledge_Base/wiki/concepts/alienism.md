# alienism

## Why It Matters
- Alienism is the historical precursor to modern psychiatry, shaping early understandings of madness and its treatment.
- The term reflects a period when psychiatry defined itself through asylum management and moral therapy, later reacting with degeneration theory.
- Understanding alienism helps illuminate the contested origins of psychiatric authority and the shift from moral to biological paradigms.

## Key Positions
| Position | Core claim | Key sources |
|----------|------------|-------------|
| Degeneration as defensive ideology | Alienists adopted degeneration theory to justify the failure of moral treatment and the chronic accumulation of asylum patients, reinterpreting madness as inherited biological deterioration. | [[008-chapter-eight]] |
| Emergence from convergence | Psychiatry (alienism) arose from a contingent convergence of mind, brain, and asylum developments, with inherent mind‑brain dualism constituting its professional identity. | [[2022 - Kendler et al. - The Emergence of Psychiatry; 1650–1850]] |
| Moral treatment foundation | French and German alienists (Pinel, Esquirol, Heinroth) established moral treatment, monomania, and the psychosomatic concept, laying foundations for modern psychiatric practice. | [[From Madness to Mental Health - 06]] |
